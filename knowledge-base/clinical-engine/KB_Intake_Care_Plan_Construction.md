**Document Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-04-03
**Classification:** Knowledge Base / Operational Protocol
**Audience:** KIMI AI Agent, Clinical Decision Support

---

## 1. Purpose — From Intake to Care Plan

### 1.1 The Care Plan is the Operational Output of Intake

The comprehensive intake assessment gathers rich, multi-dimensional data about the patient:
- **Anatomical site and clinical presentation** (location, pain pattern, provocation/relief factors)
- **Pain phenotype** (nociceptive vs. neuropathic vs. nociplastic vs. mixed)
- **Red flag screening results** (serious pathology exclusion)
- **Baseline PROMs** (pain intensity, function, psychosocial factors)
- **Psychosocial risk factors** (catastrophizing, kinesiophobia, depression, anxiety)
- **Treatment history** (prior interventions, what has/hasn't worked)
- **Patient goals and values** (what matters most to this person)

**The care plan synthesizes ALL of this data into a personalized, actionable 12-month roadmap.**

It is NOT generic. It is NOT a template. It is patient-specific, condition-specific, and risk-stratified.

### 1.2 Care Plan Requirements

The care plan must satisfy three regulatory and clinical mandates:

1. **CMS ACCESS Model Compliance**
   - A documented, evidence-based care plan must be in place within the first assessment period
   - The plan must be communicated to the patient and documented in the medical record
   - The plan must drive all subsequent MSK care decisions for 12 months

2. **Clinical Personalization and Evidence Base**
   - Evidence-based exercise prescription tailored to anatomical site, pain phenotype, and phase
   - Psychologically informed approach (CBT, pain neuroscience education) matched to patient risk profile
   - Phase-progression criteria that are objective and measurable
   - Escalation pathways defined a priori, not reactively

3. **Patient Centeredness**
   - Patient-identified goals in SMART format
   - Clear explanation of the 12-month arc ("Here's what to expect...")
   - Shared decision-making (patient agency, not prescriptive paternalism)
   - Transparent escalation criteria (what warrants specialist referral)

### 1.3 Care Plan Lifecycle

- **Creation:** Within 7 days of intake completion (ACCESS requirement)
- **Communication:** Delivered to patient in plain language with written summary
- **Tracking:** Monthly PROM reassessment; quarterly comprehensive review
- **Modification:** Phase transitions, unscheduled symptoms, escalation pathways
- **Iteration:** Updated at 3, 6, 9, 12-month marks with lessons learned
- **Closure/Transition:** 12-month outcome summary; discharge to maintenance or specialist

## 2. Care Plan Components — Required Elements

Every care plan MUST include these core components:

### 2.1 Patient Identification and Baseline Context
- **Name, MRN, Date of Birth, Assessment Date**
- **Encounter type:** Initial ACCESS enrollment
- **Care team:** Assigned care coordinator, supervising clinician

### 2.2 Primary Diagnosis and Anatomical Site
- **ICD-10 code:** Primary MSK diagnosis (e.g., M17.11 for right knee OA, M54.5 for low back pain)
- **Anatomical site:** Specific location (right knee, lumbar spine L4-5, right shoulder glenohumeral joint)
- **Pain pattern:** Localized vs. radiating, daily vs. intermittent
- **Duration:** Acute (<6 weeks), subacute (6-12 weeks), chronic (>12 weeks)

### 2.3 Pain Phenotype Classification
One of four categories (or mixed, with dominant phenotype identified):

- **Nociceptive-dominant:** Pain arising from tissue damage (OA, post-injury, tendinopathy). Improved by activity modification, loading, ROM restoration.
  - Exercise approach: Progressive tissue loading, ROM restoration, strengthening
  - Education approach: Condition-specific education, reassurance about tissue healing timeline

- **Neuropathic-dominant:** Pain with nerve involvement (radiculopathy, nerve compression, postherpetic neuralgia). Shooting, burning, electric quality; dermatomal distribution possible.
  - Exercise approach: Neural mobilization, aerobic conditioning, avoid nerve aggravation
  - Education approach: Nerve-specific education ("nerve gliding," "unhappy nerve") + PNE

- **Nociplastic-dominant:** Pain amplification without clear tissue pathology (fibromyalgia, central sensitization, chronic pain syndrome). Disproportionate pain to injury; multiple body regions; sleep disturbance.
  - Exercise approach: Time-contingent graded exposure, aerobic emphasis, pain neuroscience education integration
  - Education approach: Pain neuroscience education FIRST, normalize widespread pain, reframe as nervous system upregulation

- **Mixed:** Combination phenotype. Specify the dominant phenotype (e.g., "nociceptive-dominant with neuropathic features").

**Clinical Pearl:** The phenotype determines the exercise and education strategy more than the anatomical diagnosis.

### 2.4 Risk Stratification Level

Assign using STarT Back Screening Tool (LBP) or Keele STarT MSK (multi-site):

- **Low Risk:** STarT Back score 0-3, OR low psychosocial scores (PCS <8, TSK <17, PHQ-2 <2)
  - Typical trajectory: Phase 1 → Phase 2 (6 months) → Phase 3-4 (6-12 months)
  - Coaching: Biweekly check-ins, minimal escalation expected
  - Prognosis: 70-80% will achieve PROM improvement targets

- **Medium Risk:** STarT Back score 4-8, OR moderate psychosocial scores (PCS 8-19, TSK 17-29)
  - Typical trajectory: Phase 1 (8-12 weeks) → Phase 2 (12-20 weeks) → Phase 3-4 (remaining months)
  - Coaching: Weekly check-ins for first 8 weeks, then biweekly
  - Escalation: 20-30% may require psychology referral or additional support

- **High Risk:** STarT Back score ≥9, OR high psychosocial scores (PCS ≥20, TSK ≥30, PHQ-2 ≥2)
  - Typical trajectory: Phase 1 (12+ weeks); may plateau at Phase 2
  - Coaching: Weekly check-ins throughout, escalation to psychology/behavioral health likely
  - Escalation: 40-50% will require specialist referral (psychology, physical medicine)
  - Special considerations: CBT modules integrated early; screening for depression/anxiety at each check-in

### 2.5 PROM Baseline Scores and Improvement Targets

**Capture baseline scores from intake assessment:**

| PROM | Baseline | Phase-Based Target | MCID Threshold |
|------|----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| NRS (pain 0-10) | [patient score] | Phase-dependent (see Step 5) | 2-point reduction |
| PROMIS PF T-score | [patient score] | Phase-dependent target | 2-4 point improvement |
| PROMIS PI T-score | [patient score] | Reduce by 5-10 T-points | 5-point reduction |
| PROMIS Anxiety T-score | [if ≥60] | Reduce by 3-5 T-points | 3-point reduction |
| PROMIS Depression T-score | [if ≥60] | Reduce by 3-5 T-points | 3-point reduction |
| Condition-specific (ODI, LEFS, etc.) | [patient score] | Phase-dependent | Published MCID for instrument |
| PCS (Pain Catastrophizing) | [if score available] | Reduce by 5-10 points | - |
| TSK (Tampa Scale Kinesiophobia) | [if score available] | Reduce by 5-10 points | - |

**Reference:** Improvement targets are derived from ACCESS Model Table 6 and clinical literature benchmarks. Use the table in Section 2.5.1 to calculate patient-specific targets.

### 2.6 Exercise Prescription

**Must include:**
- **Phase assignment** (Phase 1, 2, 3, or 4 — see Step 1 algorithm)
- **Specific exercises** (3-5 exercises for first month, detailed)
- **Sets, reps, and frequency** (e.g., 3 sets x 8 reps, 2x/week, alternating days)
- **Progression criteria** (when to advance: pain tolerance, form, completion consistency)
- **Modifications** (for comorbidities, limitations, aggravating factors)
- **Timeline for progression** (when to move to next phase or next set of exercises)

Example exercise block:
```
Phase 1 — Knee OA (Right Knee Dominant)
Week 1-2 Exercises:
1. Supine quad sets — 2 sets x 10 reps, 2x/day (morning and evening)
   Cue: "Tighten thigh muscle, hold 3 seconds, relax"
   Progression: Move to single-leg quad sets when able to do 10 reps without cramping

2. Supine glute bridges — 2 sets x 8 reps, 1x/day
   Cue: "Squeeze glutes at the top, hold 2 seconds"
   Modification: Can place hands under back for support; progress to double-leg, then single-leg

3. Seated knee extension — 2 sets x 12 reps, 1x/day (alternate leg day with quad sets)
   Equipment: Chair, optional 1 lb ankle weight
   Progression: Add 1 lb weight when 12 reps painless

Dosage principle: Time-contingent (do the prescribed reps regardless of pain, within reason)
Pain response target: NRS ≤3/10 during exercise; ≤1/10 within 2 hours post-exercise
```

### 2.7 Education Plan

**Must include:**
- **PNE (Pain Neuroscience Education) sequencing** (when, which modules, dosage)
- **Condition-specific education** (disease trajectory, reassurance, activity modification)
- **CBT module sequencing** (which modules, in what order, based on psychosocial risk)
- **Dosage** (duration, frequency, format: video, live coaching, text)

Example education plan:
```
Education Timeline — Chronic Low Back Pain, High Catastrophizing (PCS = 24)

Week 1-2: Pain Neuroscience Education (PNE Module 1)
- Topic: "How Pain Works — The Nervous System Perspective"
- Format: 15-minute video + 1-page handout
- Frequency: Watch once, review handout twice
- Goal: Normalize pain response; reduce catastrophizing narrative

Week 2-3: CBT Module 4 — Unhelpful Thinking Patterns
- Topic: "Catastrophizing and Pain Thoughts"
- Format: 20-minute video + worksheet (identify patient's catastrophic thoughts)
- Frequency: Week 2 video + worksheet; Week 3 review with care coordinator
- Goal: Identify and reframe specific catastrophic thoughts

Week 4-6: Condition-Specific Education — Low Back Pain
- Topic: "Understanding Your Back Pain — What the Research Shows"
- Format: 3x 10-minute videos on movement patterns, disc herniation myths, activity tolerance
- Frequency: 1 video/week + discussion with care coordinator
- Goal: Reduce fear-avoidance; normalize activity

Month 2-3: CBT Module 1 — Relaxation Training
- Topic: "Progressive Muscle Relaxation for Pain Management"
- Format: 20-minute guided audio + practice tracking
- Frequency: 3x/week home practice, review at coaching sessions
- Goal: Reduce muscle tension; build coping skill

Ongoing: PNE Module 2 (Month 4) and Module 3 (Month 8) as pain improves
```

Reference: See KB_CBT_Pain_Psychology_Modules.md for detailed module content and dosing.

### 2.8 Coaching Cadence

**Specify frequency, type, and content:**

Example high-risk plan:
```
Months 1-2 (Phase 1 Foundation):
- Weekly check-in calls (20 minutes each) — Tuesdays, 10 AM
  Content: Exercise form check, pain monitoring, motivation, address barriers
- Weekly exercise-specific sessions (30 minutes) — Fridays
  Content: Guided exercise, live form correction, progression planning
- Monthly educational module session (20 minutes) — Third Thursday
  Content: Review education topic, Q&A, psychosocial check-in

Months 3-4 (Phase 1 → Phase 2 Transition):
- Biweekly check-in calls (20 minutes) — alternate Mondays
- Biweekly exercise coaching (30 minutes) — alternate Wednesdays
- Monthly educational module session (20 minutes) — Third Thursday
- Comprehensive review at Month 3 (60 minutes) — PROM reassessment, pain trajectory, phase readiness

Months 5-6 (Phase 2 Building):
- Biweekly check-ins, biweekly exercise coaching
- Biweekly email exercise reminders (alternate weeks without live coaching)
- Monthly educational module
- Comprehensive review at Month 6 (60 minutes)

Months 7-12 (Phase 3-4 Strengthening/Maintenance):
- Monthly check-in calls (20 minutes) — First Monday
- Monthly exercise coaching (30 minutes) — Third Friday
- Quarterly comprehensive review (60 minutes) — Months 9 and 12
- Shift to patient-directed with "check-in as needed" option
```

**Session type mix for high-risk patients:**
- Quick check-ins: 60% of interactions (phone, text, brief video)
- Exercise coaching: 20% (live form correction, progression)
- Educational modules: 15% (structured lesson + discussion)
- Comprehensive reviews: 5% (PROM data, phase readiness, escalation assessment)

### 2.9 Self-Management Goals

**Patient-identified, SMART-formatted goals tied to functional improvements:**

Example goals:
```
Patient Goal #1: "Walk 30 minutes without knee pain"
SMART Format:
- Specific: Walk continuously for 30 minutes on flat terrain (track or neighborhood)
- Measurable: Achieve NRS ≤2/10 during walk, ≤1/10 within 1 hour
- Achievable: Patient currently walks 10-15 min; progressive increase by 5 min/month
- Relevant: Patient wants to return to hiking with grandchildren
- Time-bound: Achieve by Month 6

3-Month Milestone: Walk 20 minutes at NRS ≤2/10
6-Month Milestone: Walk 30 minutes at NRS ≤2/10
12-Month Target: Walk 45 minutes, return to hiking 2-3 times/month

Patient Goal #2: "Return to strength training without flare-ups"
SMART Format:
- Specific: Perform bodyweight strength routine (squats, push-ups, rows) 2x/week
- Measurable: Complete 3 sets x 8-10 reps, pain ≤1/10 during, no flare-up (NRS increase >2 points) within 24 hours
- Achievable: Patient was active before injury; structured progression from Phase 1 will support
- Relevant: Patient identity as "active person" and fitness maintenance
- Time-bound: Return to strength training by Month 9

3-Month Milestone: Tolerate 15-minute bodyweight workout at NRS ≤2/10
6-Month Milestone: Perform full routine 2x/week at NRS ≤1/10
12-Month Target: Strength training sustainable; return to weights if desired
```

Goals should reflect patient values, not clinician prescriptions. Review and refine at each quarterly review.

### 2.10 Escalation Thresholds

**Patient-specific triggers for unscheduled intervention or specialist referral:**

Example escalation criteria:
```
ESCALATION THRESHOLD #1: Pain Increase
- IF NRS increases >2 points from baseline AND persists >1 week → Schedule unscheduled review call
- IF NRS increases >3 points at ANY time → Immediate escalation review (same week)
- IF pain accompanied by new neurological symptoms (numbness, weakness) → Red flag protocol (see KB_Red_Flag_Screening.md)

ESCALATION THRESHOLD #2: Functional Decline
- IF PROMIS PF score decreases >4 T-points from previous assessment → Review barriers; assess overload vs. underactivity
- IF patient reports inability to perform baseline self-care tasks (ADLs) → Urgent review; consider Phase de-escalation

ESCALATION THRESHOLD #3: Psychosocial Deterioration
- IF PHQ-2 score ≥3 (depression positive) or GAD-2 score ≥3 (anxiety positive) → Referral to psychology/behavioral health
- IF PCS score increases >5 points from baseline → Escalation: CBT intensification + psychology consult
- IF patient reports suicidal ideation or safety concerns at ANY point → Immediate crisis protocol

ESCALATION THRESHOLD #4: Medication Changes or New Comorbidities
- IF patient reports new opioid use or dose increase → Escalation to physician; pain management review
- IF patient reports new cardiac symptoms, new fracture concern, or other red flag → Redirect to acute care pathway

ESCALATION THRESHOLD #5: Lack of Progress at Phase Transition
- IF patient does not achieve PROM improvement targets by Month 3 → Extended Phase 1; reassess barriers
- IF patient remains in Phase 1 beyond Month 4 without PROMIS PF improvement ≥2 T-points → Escalation to PT/physiatry for clinical reassessment

De-escalation (relaxation of coaching frequency):
- IF patient meets all PROM targets AND reports independent exercise compliance for 4+ weeks → May reduce check-in frequency
- IF patient progresses through Phase 3 and pain stable (NRS <2) → Shift to monthly check-ins
```

### 2.11 Reassessment Schedule

**Scheduled touchpoints for PROM reassessment and care plan review:**

```
Monthly PROM Reassessment (via patient-reported or clinician-administered):
- NRS pain score
- PROMIS Physical Function (or condition-specific functional measure)
- Check-in question: "How is your pain this month compared to last month?"

Quarterly Comprehensive Review (Month 3, 6, 9, 12):
- All baseline PROMs re-administered
- Pain phenotype re-evaluation (has it shifted?)
- Risk stratification re-score (STarT Back or Keele STarT MSK)
- Psychosocial screening (PHQ-2, GAD-2, PCS, TSK if not done recently)
- Exercise tolerance and form assessment
- Goal progress review
- Phase transition readiness assessment
- Care plan modifications

Red Flag Reassessment:
- IF patient reports new red flag symptoms → Immediate re-screening (see KB_Red_Flag_Screening.md)
- IF symptoms change location or character → Pain phenotype re-evaluation

Patient-Requested Reassessment:
- Patient may request review at any time; documented in care plan updates
```

## 3. Care Plan Construction Algorithm

This is the **step-by-step decision logic** for building a patient-specific care plan from intake data. An LLM should be able to follow each if-then rule to construct a complete plan.

### Step 1: Determine Phase Assignment

**Input data:**
- NRS pain score (0-10)
- PROMIS PF T-score (or equivalent functional measure baseline)
- Acuity (recent flare vs. chronic stable)
- Prior treatment level (new to structured care vs. already been through PT)

**Decision tree:**

```
IF (NRS ≥7 OR PROMIS PF T-score <35 OR patient is "new to structured exercise") THEN
  → PHASE 1 (Foundation)
  Rationale: High pain, significant functional limitation, or no exercise baseline
  Duration: 6-12 weeks typically; may extend if PROM progress slow

ELSE IF (NRS 4-6 AND PROMIS PF T-score 35-42 AND patient tolerating initial exercises for ≥2 weeks) THEN
  → PHASE 2 (Building)
  Rationale: Moderate pain with early functional recovery

ELSE IF (NRS 2-4 AND PROMIS PF T-score 42-50 AND patient independent with Phase 2 exercises) THEN
  → PHASE 3 (Strengthening)
  Rationale: Mild pain with good functional capacity

ELSE IF (NRS <3 AND PROMIS PF T-score ≥50 AND patient functionally independent) THEN
  → PHASE 4 (Maintenance)
  Rationale: Pain resolved or near-resolved; focus on maintenance and return to activity

DEFAULT: Start with PHASE 1 for new ACCESS enrollees
Note: Most patients enter ACCESS at Phase 1 or Phase 2.
```

**Output:** One phase assignment (e.g., "Phase 1: Foundation — 8-week duration estimated")

### Step 2: Select Exercise Prescription

**Input data:**
- Anatomical site (knee, hip, spine, shoulder, etc.)
- Pain phenotype (nociceptive, neuropathic, nociplastic, mixed)
- Phase assignment (from Step 1)
- Comorbidities (cardiac, balance issues, obesity, etc.)

**Decision tree:**

```
STEP 2A: Select Exercise Library Based on Anatomical Site
IF anatomical site = "knee" THEN → Knee Exercise Library (quadriceps, glute, hamstring focused)
IF anatomical site = "hip" THEN → Hip Exercise Library (glute med, external rotators, hip flexor focused)
IF anatomical site = "lumbar spine" THEN → Lumbar Spine Exercise Library (core stability, hip hinge, neutral spine emphasized)
IF anatomical site = "cervical spine" THEN → Cervical Spine Exercise Library (neck mobility, scapular stability, posture focused)
IF anatomical site = "shoulder" THEN → Shoulder Exercise Library (rotator cuff, scapular, ROM)
IF anatomical site = "elbow" or "wrist" THEN → Upper extremity Exercise Library
IF anatomical site = "ankle" or "foot" THEN → Lower extremity Exercise Library
IF anatomical site = "multi-site" or "whole body" THEN → General MSK Exercise Library (aerobic emphasis)

STEP 2B: Select Exercise Approach Based on Pain Phenotype

IF pain phenotype = "nociceptive-dominant" THEN
  → Exercise approach: Progressive tissue loading, ROM restoration, strengthening emphasis
  → Loading progression: Light → Moderate → Heavy (as tolerated)
  → Example: Squat progression from partial-ROM → full ROM → weighted; isometric hold → dynamic movement
  → Pain tolerance: Can tolerate some pain during exercise (NRS 3-4/10 acceptable during reps; return to baseline within 2 hours)

ELSE IF pain phenotype = "neuropathic-dominant" THEN
  → Exercise approach: Neural mobilization, aerobic conditioning, AVOID nerve aggravation
  → Specific additions: Nerve gliding exercises, neural flossing if applicable
  → Aerobic emphasis: 20-30 min low-impact aerobic 3-4x/week (walking, cycling, swimming)
  → Pain tolerance: More conservative; keep pain ≤2/10 during exercise to avoid sensitization
  → Example: If radiculopathy, add seated cat-camel for lumbar mobility; if peripheral nerve, add ROM + aerobic

ELSE IF pain phenotype = "nociplastic-dominant" THEN
  → Exercise approach: Time-contingent graded exposure, AEROBIC EMPHASIS, pain neuroscience education integration
  → Specific additions: Aerobic exercise prioritized; structured graded exposure to fear-inducing activities
  → Dosage: "Do this many reps/this long regardless of pain" (time-contingent, not pain-contingent)
  → Education: PNE Module 1 integrated early (Week 1-2) alongside exercise
  → Pain tolerance: "Exercise is safe even if painful; pain does not mean harm"
  → Example: 20-30 min aerobic conditioning 3-4x/week; add tolerance-building movements (e.g., tolerate arm movement despite arm pain)

ELSE IF pain phenotype = "mixed" THEN
  → Determine dominant phenotype (e.g., "nociceptive-dominant with neuropathic features")
  → Follow the dominant phenotype approach above
  → Adjust: Include elements of non-dominant phenotypes (e.g., nociceptive-dominant + neural mobilization component; nociplastic-dominant + some loading for any OA component)

STEP 2C: Apply Phase-Specific Exercise Selection

PHASE 1 (Foundation):
- Select 3-4 exercises from the anatomical library
- Emphasis: Isometric, low-ROM, bodyweight or assisted
- Frequency: Daily or 6 days/week
- Duration: 10-15 minutes
- Pain response: NRS 0-2/10 during exercise; may allow 3-4/10 for short bursts
- Example Knee OA Phase 1: Quad sets, glute bridges, seated knee extension, supine hip abduction

PHASE 2 (Building):
- Progress exercises from Phase 1; add 1-2 new exercises
- Emphasis: Partial ROM → full ROM; lighter load → moderate load
- Frequency: 4-5 days/week
- Duration: 15-25 minutes
- Pain response: NRS ≤2/10 during exercise
- Example Knee OA Phase 2: Progress to single-leg quad sets, add mini-squats, add step-ups, lunges

PHASE 3 (Strengthening):
- Introduce loading, dynamic movements, sport/activity-specific
- Emphasis: Heavy loading, sport simulation, power if appropriate
- Frequency: 3-4 days/week (resistance) + 2-3 days/week aerobic
- Duration: 25-40 minutes
- Pain response: NRS ≤1/10 during exercise
- Example Knee OA Phase 3: Full squats (with weight), step-ups with weight, single-leg balance + arm movement, jump-ready movements

PHASE 4 (Maintenance):
- Patient-directed; maintenance exercises + return to sport/activity
- Emphasis: Self-management, injury prevention, activity tolerance
- Frequency: 2-3 days/week resistance; 3-4 days/week aerobic
- Duration: 30-45 minutes
- Pain response: Asymptomatic
- Example Knee OA Phase 4: Patient-chosen strength program, return to hiking/recreation, balance training

STEP 2D: Apply Comorbidity Modifications

IF comorbidity = "balance issues or fall risk" THEN
  → Provide seated alternatives for standing exercises
  → Add standing balance component (hold kitchen counter) before progressing
  → Avoid tandem stance or single-leg until balance improved

IF comorbidity = "cardiac history" THEN
  → Obtain clearance from cardiologist before heavy loading
  → Monitor HR during exercise; stay below age-predicted max if any contraindication
  → Prefer aerobic (walking, cycling) over heavy resistance initially

IF comorbidity = "obesity (BMI >30)" THEN
  → Reduce impact (prefer non-impact aerobic: cycling, swimming, elliptical)
  → Modify ROM to patient tolerance
  → Reduce reps/sets; progress slower

IF comorbidity = "recent surgery (within 6 months)" THEN
  → Respect surgical precautions (e.g., no heavy loading after ACL reconstruction until 6+ months)
  → Start at more conservative phase; progress based on surgical protocol

OUTPUT: Specific 3-5 exercise prescription with sets, reps, frequency, and 4-week progression plan
```

### Step 3: Design Education Sequence

**Input data:**
- Pain phenotype
- Psychosocial risk scores (PCS, TSK, PHQ-2, GAD-2)
- Patient health literacy (if known)
- Treatment history

**Decision tree:**

```
STEP 3A: Determine PNE (Pain Neuroscience Education) Priority

IF pain phenotype = "nociplastic-dominant" THEN
  → PNE PRIORITY: Deliver Module 1 (Pain Neuroscience Basics) in Weeks 1-2
  Rationale: Central sensitization; patient needs to understand pain amplification mechanism

ELSE IF pain phenotype = "neuropathic-dominant" THEN
  → PNE PRIORITY: Moderate; deliver after nerve-specific education
  Sequence: Week 1-2 nerve-specific education → Week 3-4 PNE Module 1 → Week 5-6 PNE Module 2 (nerve sensitization)

ELSE IF pain phenotype = "nociceptive-dominant" THEN
  → PNE PRIORITY: Lower; deliver after condition-specific education
  Sequence: Week 1-2 condition-specific education → Week 4-6 PNE Module 1 → Month 3+ PNE Module 2-3

STEP 3B: Determine CBT Module Sequencing Based on Psychosocial Risk

IF PCS (Pain Catastrophizing Scale) score ≥20 THEN
  → CBT Module 4 (Unhelpful Thinking Patterns) → Early intervention (Weeks 2-3)
  → Follow with Module 3 (Activity Pacing) in Weeks 4-5
  → Rationale: Catastrophic thinking loops are driving pain; need early cognitive restructuring

ELSE IF PCS score 10-19 THEN
  → CBT Module 4 → Weeks 4-6 (moderate priority)
  → Follow with other modules as below

ELSE IF PCS score <10 THEN
  → Defer CBT Module 4; focus on other modules

IF TSK (Tampa Scale Kinesiophobia) score ≥30 THEN
  → CBT Module 5 (Graded Exposure to Fear-Inducing Activities) → Early intervention (Weeks 3-4)
  → Include exposure hierarchy in exercise progression
  → Rationale: Fear-avoidance is preventing activity engagement; need structured exposure

ELSE IF TSK score 17-29 THEN
  → CBT Module 5 → Weeks 6-8 (moderate priority)

ELSE IF TSK score <17 THEN
  → Defer Module 5; focus on others

IF PHQ-2 (Depression) score ≥2 THEN
  → CBT Module 1 (Relaxation Training) + Module 2 (Breathing Techniques) → Weeks 1-2
  → Consider referral to mental health provider if PHQ-9 ≥10
  → Rationale: Depression screening positive; need coping skills + professional assessment

ELSE IF PHQ-2 score 1 THEN
  → CBT Module 1-2 → Weeks 3-4 (lower priority)

ELSE IF PHQ-2 score 0 THEN
  → Defer Modules 1-2; focus on others

IF GAD-2 (Anxiety) score ≥2 THEN
  → CBT Module 2 (Breathing Techniques, Relaxation) → Weeks 1-2
  → Consider referral to mental health provider if GAD-7 ≥10
  → Rationale: Anxiety screening positive; need immediate coping skills

ELSE IF GAD-2 score <2 THEN
  → Defer Module 2; focus on others

STEP 3C: Build the 12-Month Education Timeline (Reference: KB_CBT_Pain_Psychology_Modules.md)

Example timeline for HIGH-RISK patient (high catastrophizing, kinesiophobia, depression positive):

**Months 1-2 (Weeks 1-8):**
- Week 1: PNE Module 1 video + handout (or condition-specific education if nociceptive-dominant)
- Week 2: CBT Module 4 (Unhelpful Thinking Patterns) video + worksheet
- Week 3: CBT Module 5 (Graded Exposure) introduction; build exposure hierarchy
- Week 4-5: CBT Module 1 (Relaxation Training) — guided practice 3x/week
- Week 6: Review all modules; patient self-assessment of insights

**Months 3-4 (Weeks 9-16):**
- Week 9: PNE Module 2 (Neuroplasticity and Pain) if first module well-received
- Week 12: CBT Module 3 (Pacing and Pacing Strategies) — prevent boom-bust cycles
- Week 14: Condition-specific education part 2 (if not fully covered in Month 1)

**Months 5-6 (Weeks 17-24):**
- Consolidation: Review previous modules with care coordinator
- Week 20: PNE Module 3 (Recovery and Resilience) if patient progressing well
- Ongoing: Graded exposure homework; pain neuroscience principles reinforced in coaching

**Months 7-12:**
- Maintenance: Monthly review of previous modules
- Month 9: Advanced topics (e.g., sleep, stress management, pain neuroscience in sport)
- Ongoing: Patient autonomy emphasized; self-directed learning encouraged

Example timeline for LOW-RISK patient (minimal psychosocial risk):

**Months 1-2:**
- Week 1-2: Condition-specific education (disease trajectory, activity modification)
- Week 3-4: PNE Module 1 (brief version)

**Months 3-4:**
- Month 3: Brief CBT check-in (Module 2 — relaxation, if interested); mostly optional

**Months 5-12:**
- Self-directed learning; brief monthly education reminders

OUTPUT: Detailed 12-month education calendar with specific module, week, format, duration, and learning objective for each
```

### Step 4: Set Coaching Cadence

**Input data:**
- Risk stratification (Low, Medium, High)
- Phase assignment
- Patient availability and preference
- Comorbidities (e.g., depression, anxiety)

**Decision tree:**

```
IF risk stratification = "HIGH RISK" (STarT Back ≥9, or high psychosocial scores) THEN

  Months 1-2 (Phase 1 Intensive):
  - Weekly check-in calls: 20 minutes, synchronous (e.g., Tuesdays 10 AM)
    Content: Pain monitoring, exercise form check, motivation, barrier identification
  - Weekly exercise coaching: 30 minutes, live video or phone
    Content: Live exercise demonstration, form correction, progression planning
  - Monthly educational module session: 20 minutes, group or individual
    Content: Module review, Q&A, psychosocial check-in
  - Monthly comprehensive review: 60 minutes, in-person if possible or video
    Content: Full PROM reassessment, phase readiness, escalation assessment
```

### Step 5: Establish PROM Improvement Targets

**Input data:**
- All baseline PROM scores (NRS, PROMIS PF, PROMIS PI, condition-specific, etc.)
- Patient's phase assignment
- Pain phenotype

**Decision tree:**

```
FOR EACH BASELINE PROM SCORE:

  IF PROM = "NRS (Numeric Rating Scale, 0-10)" THEN
    Phase 1 (High Pain, NRS baseline 7-10):
      3-Month Target: Reduce by 2 points (e.g., 8 → 6)
      6-Month Target: Reduce by 3-4 points (e.g., 8 → 4)
      12-Month Target: NRS <3/10 or 50% reduction from baseline

    Phase 2 (Moderate Pain, NRS baseline 4-6):
      3-Month Target: Reduce by 1-2 points (e.g., 5 → 3)
      6-Month Target: Reduce by 2-3 points (e.g., 5 → 2)
      12-Month Target: NRS <2/10

    Phase 3-4 (Low Pain, NRS baseline <4):
      3-Month Target: Maintain or reduce by 0-1 point
      6-Month Target: Maintain <2/10
      12-Month Target: Pain-free or minimal (NRS 0-1/10)

    MCID (Minimum Clinically Important Difference): 2-point reduction
    Calculation: If baseline NRS = 7, target for 12 months = ≤4 (or 7-3=4)

  ELSE IF PROM = "PROMIS Physical Function (T-score)" THEN
    T-score interpretation: Mean 50, SD 10 (national norm)
    - T-score <35: Substantial functional limitation
    - T-score 35-42: Moderate functional limitation
    - T-score 42-50: Mild functional limitation
    - T-score >50: At or above normal functioning

    Phase 1 (T-score baseline <35):
      3-Month Target: Increase by 2 T-points (e.g., 32 → 34)
      6-Month Target: Increase by 4 T-points (e.g., 32 → 36)
      12-Month Target: T-score ≥42 (mild limitation or better)

    Phase 2 (T-score baseline 35-42):
      3-Month Target: Increase by 2-3 T-points (e.g., 38 → 41)
      6-Month Target: Increase by 4-5 T-points (e.g., 38 → 43)
      12-Month Target: T-score >45

    Phase 3-4 (T-score baseline >42):
      3-Month Target: Maintain or increase by 1-2 T-points
      12-Month Target: T-score ≥50 (normal functioning)

    MCID: 2-4 T-point improvement (depends on baseline, typically 3-4 points)

  ELSE IF PROM = "PROMIS Pain Interference (T-score)" THEN
    T-score interpretation: Higher score = worse pain interference

    Phase 1 (T-score baseline >65):
      3-Month Target: Reduce by 3-5 T-points
      6-Month Target: Reduce by 5-8 T-points
      12-Month Target: T-score <55 (minimal interference)

    Phase 2 (T-score baseline 55-65):
      3-Month Target: Reduce by 2-3 T-points
      12-Month Target: T-score <50 (near-normal)

    MCID: 5-point reduction

  ELSE IF PROM = "PROMIS Anxiety (T-score)" (If baseline ≥60) THEN
    3-Month Target: Reduce by 2-3 T-points
    6-Month Target: Reduce by 4-5 T-points
    12-Month Target: T-score <55 (minimal anxiety)
    MCID: 3-point reduction
    Note: If PHQ-2 or GAD-2 positive at intake, prioritize mental health referral alongside targets

  ELSE IF PROM = "PROMIS Depression (T-score)" (If baseline ≥60) THEN
    3-Month Target: Reduce by 2-3 T-points
    6-Month Target: Reduce by 4-5 T-points
    12-Month Target: T-score <55 (minimal depression)
    MCID: 3-point reduction
    Note: If PHQ-2 positive at intake, referral to mental health provider is MANDATORY

  ELSE IF PROM = "Condition-Specific Measure" (ODI, LEFS, PSFS, etc.) THEN

    IF measure = "ODI (Oswestry Disability Index, 0-100)" THEN
      MCID: 8-10 point reduction
      Phase 1 (high baseline >50):
        3-Month Target: Reduce by 8-10 points (e.g., 60 → 50)
        6-Month Target: Reduce by 15-20 points (e.g., 60 → 40)
        12-Month Target: <30 (mild disability) or 50% improvement

    ELSE IF measure = "LEFS (Lower Extremity Functional Scale, 0-80)" THEN
      MCID: 9-12 point increase (improvement = higher score)
      Phase 1 (low baseline <30):
        3-Month Target: Increase by 8-12 points (e.g., 20 → 32)
        6-Month Target: Increase by 15-20 points (e.g., 20 → 40)
        12-Month Target: >60 (minimal disability)

    ELSE IF measure = "PSFS (Patient-Specific Functional Scale, 0-10 per item)" THEN
      MCID: 2-3 point improvement per item
      Goal: Increase each item by 2-3 points on average (e.g., 3/10 → 6-7/10)
      12-Month Target: >7/10 on each self-identified goal item

    ELSE (Other condition-specific measure):
      Use published MCID for that instrument (reference in care plan notes)
      Apply to baseline to calculate targets

  ELSE IF PROM = "PCS (Pain Catastrophizing Scale, 0-52)" (If captured at intake) THEN
    MCID: Not formally established; use 5-point reduction as clinical milestone
    Phase 1 (high catastrophizing, baseline >20):
      3-Month Target: Reduce by 3-5 points (e.g., 25 → 20)
      6-Month Target: Reduce by 5-10 points (e.g., 25 → 15)
      12-Month Target: <15 (low catastrophizing)
    Note: PCS reduction is SECONDARY to NRS/functional measure improvement

  ELSE IF PROM = "TSK (Tampa Scale Kinesiophobia, 17-68)" (If captured at intake) THEN
    MCID: Not formally established; use 5-point reduction as clinical milestone
    Phase 1 (high fear-avoidance, baseline ≥30):
      3-Month Target: Reduce by 3-5 points
      6-Month Target: Reduce by 5-10 points (e.g., 35 → 25)
      12-Month Target: <25 (low kinesiophobia)
    Note: TSK reduction is SECONDARY to NRS/functional measure improvement

Interim Assessment Schedule:
- Month 1: NRS only (brief check)
- Month 3: Full PROM reassessment; compare to targets
- Month 6: Full PROM reassessment; compare to targets; phase transition decision
- Month 9: Full PROM reassessment; trajectory assessment
- Month 12: Full PROM reassessment; discharge assessment

OUTPUT: Patient-specific numeric targets for each PROM at 3, 6, 9, and 12-month marks
Example: "Patient baseline NRS = 8, target at 3 months = 6 (2-point reduction), target at 12 months = ≤3 (≥5-point reduction or 50% improvement)"
```

### Step 6: Define Patient-Centered Goals

**Input data:**
- Patient interview notes (functional goals, values, priorities)
- Baseline functional status (PROMIS, condition-specific measures)
- Clinical context (diagnosis, severity)

**Decision tree:**

```
EXTRACT PATIENT-IDENTIFIED FUNCTIONAL GOALS from intake interview:
Examples:
- "Walk with my grandkids without pain"
- "Return to yoga"
- "Sleep through the night"
- "Work at my desk without taking breaks"
- "Play tennis again"
- "Get on and off the floor independently"

FOR EACH PATIENT GOAL:

  SMART-Format Conversion:

  Specific: Break the goal into specific, measurable actions
    Input: "Walk with my grandkids"
    Output: "Walk continuously on flat terrain (neighborhood or park) for [patient-specified duration or distance]"

  Measurable: Tie to objective benchmark or PROM
    Input: "Walk with grandkids without pain"
    Output: "NRS ≤2/10 during walk, ≤1/10 within 1 hour post-walk; walk duration [X minutes]"
    OR: "LEFS score ≥65 (able to walk 30+ minutes)"

  Achievable: Clinically realistic given severity and timeline
    Input: Current status (e.g., "walks 10 minutes, NRS 5/10")
    Assessment: Achievable if progression is 5 min/month → reach 30 min by month 4
    Output: "Walk 30 minutes at NRS ≤2/10 by Month 6" (realistic from 10 min baseline)

  Relevant: Matters to the patient (not clinician-imposed)
    INPUT: Patient's own words from interview
    OUTPUT: Ensure goal reflects patient values ("identity as active person," "family participation," etc.)

  Time-Bound: Specific timeline with milestones
    Input: Patient wants "to be back to normal"
    Output: "Return to walking 30 min by Month 6; return to grandkid outings 2x/month by Month 9; return to full hiking by Month 12"
    Include: 3-month, 6-month, 9-month, 12-month specific targets

GOAL REFINEMENT RULES:

IF patient goal is vague (e.g., "get better," "reduce pain") THEN
  → Ask clarifying questions in intake interview:
    "What specific activity would you like to do pain-free?"
    "How often do you want to do this activity?"
    "How long can you currently do this before pain stops you?"
  → Convert vague goal to specific functional goal

IF patient goal is unrealistic (e.g., "return to competitive athletics" when 75-year-old with severe OA) THEN
  → Acknowledge the goal
  → Reframe into realistic milestone:
    "That's a great goal. Let's start with pain-free walking 20 minutes, then build toward gentle hiking. That's often where patients your age with knee arthritis land in the first year."
  → Set modified goal and reassess at Month 6

IF patient has no clear goals ("I just want my pain gone") THEN
  → Suggest functional goals based on baseline limitations:
    "I notice you have difficulty with stairs. Would returning to stair climbing be meaningful for you?"
    "Would you like to return to a specific activity you did before your pain started?"
  → Establish at least 2-3 goals collaboratively

DOCUMENT GOALS IN PLAIN LANGUAGE:
Example for LBP patient:
"Patient Goal #1: Return to comfortable desk work without flare-ups
  - Currently: Can sit 30 minutes before back pain (NRS 4/10), must stand every 20 min
  - 3-Month Target: Sit 1 hour comfortably, NRS ≤1/10 while working
  - 6-Month Target: Sit 2+ hours with one movement break; pain not interfering with work concentration
  - 12-Month Target: Sit 3-4 hours without pain; desk work sustainable full-time
  - Measurement: Patient report + PROMIS Physical Function score (expect 4-6 T-point improvement)

Patient Goal #2: Return to walking 5 km without pain
  - Currently: Walks 1-2 km before pain increases to NRS 5-6/10
  - 3-Month Target: Walk 2 km at NRS ≤2/10
  - 6-Month Target: Walk 3-4 km at NRS ≤1/10
  - 12-Month Target: Walk 5 km pain-free (patient's pre-pain activity level)
  - Measurement: Timed walk distance + NRS monitoring

Patient Goal #3: Sleep through the night
  - Currently: Wakes 2-3x per night due to pain; total sleep 5-6 hours
  - 3-Month Target: Wakes 1x per night; total sleep 6-7 hours
  - 6-Month Target: Wakes 0-1x per night; total sleep 7+ hours
  - 12-Month Target: Sleeps through most nights (6-7 out of 7 nights)
  - Measurement: Patient sleep diary + PROMIS Sleep score if available
  - Note: May require sleep education or psychology consult if sleep issue persists despite pain reduction"

OUTPUT: 2-3 SMART-formatted patient goals with specific 3-month, 6-month, and 12-month milestones; linked to measurable PROM or functional outcomes
```

### Step 7: Set Escalation Thresholds

**Input data:**
- Risk stratification (Low, Medium, High)
- Pain phenotype
- Baseline psychosocial risk (depression, anxiety, catastrophizing, kinesiophobia)

**Decision tree:**

```
ESCALATION THRESHOLD #1: Pain Increase (Applies to All Risk Levels)

IF NRS increases >2 points from baseline AND persists for >1 week THEN
  → Schedule unscheduled review call within 48 hours
  → Assess: New activity, overload, flare pattern, medication changes
  → Action: Modify exercise load OR extend Phase 1

ELSE IF NRS increases >3 points from baseline at ANY time THEN
  → Urgent escalation review (same week)
  → Assess for new red flag symptoms (see KB_Red_Flag_Screening.md)
  → Action: May require PT or physician re-evaluation

ELSE IF pain accompanied by NEW neurological symptoms (new numbness, weakness, bowel/bladder changes) THEN
  → IMMEDIATE RED FLAG PROTOCOL (see KB_Red_Flag_Screening.md)
  → Escalate to physician same day

ESCALATION THRESHOLD #2: Functional Decline (Applies to All Risk Levels)

IF PROMIS Physical Function T-score decreases >4 T-points from previous assessment THEN
  → Schedule review call within 48 hours
  → Assess: Exercise overload? Flare? Lack of activity? Comorbidity?
  → Action: Modify exercise; may de-escalate phase

ELSE IF patient reports NEW inability to perform baseline ADLs (dressing, bathing, toileting) THEN
  → Urgent escalation (within 48 hours)
  → Assess: Flare vs. disease progression vs. other acute illness
  → Action: May require Phase de-escalation and PT reassessment

ESCALATION THRESHOLD #3: Psychosocial Deterioration (Varies by Risk Level)

HIGH RISK PATIENTS (baseline depression/anxiety positive):
  IF PHQ-2 score ≥3 (depression screening positive) at ANY reassessment THEN
    → MANDATORY referral to mental health provider (psychology or psychiatry)
    → Time-frame: Referral within 1 week
    → MSK care continues in parallel

  IF GAD-2 score ≥3 (anxiety screening positive) at ANY reassessment THEN
    → MANDATORY referral to mental health provider
    → Time-frame: Referral within 1 week
    → Reduce coaching intensity if anxiety interfering with exercise compliance

  IF patient reports suicidal ideation or safety concerns THEN
    → IMMEDIATE CRISIS PROTOCOL
    → Do not continue coaching; escalate to crisis intervention
    → Contact emergency services if acute risk

MEDIUM/LOW RISK PATIENTS:
  IF PHQ-2 scores ≥3 on reassessment THEN
    → Recommend mental health referral (not mandatory, but encouraged)
    → Time-frame: Within 2-4 weeks
    → MSK care continues; may modify CBT module sequencing

  IF patient-reported mood decline or anhedonia noted in coaching calls THEN
    → Screening conversation at next call
    → If concerning, escalate to physician for PHQ-9

CATASTROPHIZING ESCALATION:
  IF PCS score increases >5 points from baseline OR >10 points absolute THEN
    → Escalation: Intensify CBT Module 4 (Unhelpful Thinking)
    → Add weekly psychology consult if not already engaged
    → Timeline: Increase coaching to weekly if had been biweekly

KINESIOPHOBIA ESCALATION:
  IF TSK score increases >5 points from baseline THEN
    → Escalation: Intensify CBT Module 5 (Graded Exposure)
    → Modify exercise prescription: Make movements LESS scary, not more
    → Add psychology consult if TSK remains >35 despite intervention

ESCALATION THRESHOLD #4: Medication Changes or New Comorbidities

IF patient reports new opioid use or dose increase THEN
  → Escalation to physician within 1 week
  → Pain management review: Is pain control adequate? Why escalating opioids?
  → MSK care may need pause pending pain medicine assessment

IF patient reports new health change (chest pain, shortness of breath, new joint swelling, fracture concern) THEN
  → Redirect to acute care pathway immediately
  → Pause routine MSK coaching pending medical clearance
  → Resume once acute issue ruled out

ESCALATION THRESHOLD #5: Lack of Progress at Phase Transition

IF patient does NOT achieve PROM improvement targets by Month 3 (e.g., no NRS reduction, PROMIS PF unchanged) THEN
  → Escalation: Extended Phase 1 (do not advance to Phase 2)
  → Assess barriers:
    - Inadequate exercise compliance? → Modify frequency/intensity
    - Pain phenotype misclassification? → Reassess; may need different approach
    - Psychosocial block? → Increase psychology support
    - Structural issue (OA severity, stenosis)? → PT or imaging reassessment
  → Revised timeline: Target Month 4-5 for Phase 2 advance if barriers addressed

ELSE IF patient remains in Phase 1 beyond 12 weeks WITHOUT PROMIS PF improvement ≥2 T-points THEN
  → Escalation to PT/physiatry for clinical reassessment
  → Consider imaging or advanced diagnostics if not yet done
  → Time-frame: Schedule within 2-4 weeks

DE-ESCALATION (Relaxation of Coaching Intensity):
  When patient is doing WELL and can step down support:

  IF patient meets all PROM targets AND reports independent exercise compliance for 4+ weeks consecutive THEN
    → May reduce check-in frequency by one step (e.g., weekly → biweekly)
    → Patient confirms readiness; not clinician-imposed
    → Maintain "call if needed" option

  IF patient progresses through Phase 3 AND pain stable (NRS <2/10) for 4+ weeks THEN
    → Shift to monthly check-ins (from biweekly)
    → Shift exercise coaching to monthly (offer weekly email reminders if desired)
    → Patient increasingly self-directed

  IF patient reaches Phase 4 maintenance AND continues independent compliance for 8+ weeks THEN
    → Shift to quarterly check-ins (every 3 months)
    → Exercise coaching on-demand only
    → Prepare for discharge/transition to self-management

OUTPUT: Patient-specific escalation thresholds with trigger values, action items, and time-frames (e.g., "If NRS increases >2 points for >1 week, schedule review within 48 hours")
```

### 4.1 Knee Osteoarthritis — Typical Initial Care Plan

**Patient Profile:** 68-year-old female; right knee OA; baseline NRS 7/10, PROMIS PF T-score 32; overweight (BMI 31); moderate risk (STarT Back equivalent 5)

**Exercise Prescription:**

1. **Supine quadriceps sets** — 2 sets x 10 reps, 2x/day (morning, evening)
   - Cue: "Tighten thigh, hold 3 seconds, relax"
   - Progression criteria: Move to single-leg quad sets when able without cramping

2. **Supine glute bridges** — 2 sets x 8 reps, 1x/day
   - Cue: "Squeeze glutes at top, hold 2 seconds"
   - Modification: Hands under back for support; progress to double-leg, then single-leg

3. **Seated knee extension** — 2 sets x 12 reps, 1x/day (alternate days with quad sets)
   - Equipment: Chair, optional 1 lb ankle weight
   - Progression: Add weight when 12 reps achieved painlessly

4. **Supine hip abduction (side-lying leg lifts)** — 2 sets x 10 reps, 1x/day
   - Cue: "Lift top leg straight up, hold 2 seconds"
   - Progression: Add 1 lb ankle weight after 2 weeks

5. **Stationary cycling (if available)** — 10-15 minutes, 3-4x/week
   - Seat height adjusted so knee bends ~20 degrees at extension
   - Progression: Increase duration by 2-3 min/week

**Dosage principle:** Time-contingent (do prescribed reps; pain tolerance: NRS ≤3/10 during, return to baseline within 2 hours)

**Coaching Cadence:**
- Weekly check-in calls (Tuesdays 10 AM, 20 min)
- Biweekly exercise coaching (live video form check, Fridays)
- Monthly comprehensive review

**Education Plan:**
- Week 1-2: Condition-specific education ("What is knee OA and how exercise helps")
- Week 3-4: PNE Module 1 (brief: "Pain from knee OA is tissue damage, but most respond to loading")
- Week 6-8: CBT Module 2 (relaxation breathing for pain flare-ups)

**PROM Targets:**
- NRS: Baseline 7 → Target 6 at 3 months, ≤4 at 6 months, ≤3 at 12 months
- PROMIS PF: Baseline 32 → Target 34 at 3 months, 36-38 at 6 months, ≥42 at 12 months

1. "Walk 30 minutes on flat terrain without pain"
   - 3-month: Walk 15 min at NRS ≤2/10
   - 6-month: Walk 25 min at NRS ≤2/10
   - 12-month: Walk 30-40 min at NRS ≤1/10

2. "Return to gardening 2 hours at a time"
   - 3-month: Tolerate 30 min kneeling/crouching at NRS ≤2/10
   - 6-month: Tolerate 1 hour with one position break
   - 12-month: 2 hours gardening without significant pain

**Escalation Thresholds:**
- NRS >9/10 → urgent review (same week)
- No NRS improvement by Week 6 → re-assess exercise load, consider PT evaluation

- Mood decline (PHQ-2 ≥2) → mental health referral

**Phase Progression Criteria (toward Phase 2 at ~Week 8):**
- NRS ≤6/10 consistently
- PROMIS PF improvement ≥2 T-points
- Able to perform all Phase 1 exercises with good form, pain ≤2/10
- Independent exercise compliance ≥4/week

1. "Return to desk work without flare-ups" — Target 3+ hours by Month 6

3. "Sleep through the night" — Target 1x wake or less by Month 6

**Escalation Thresholds:**
- NRS >9/10 → urgent review
- New radicular symptoms (leg pain, numbness) → red flag protocol
- PCS increase >5 points → escalate CBT intensity

### 4.4 Cervical Spondylosis / Neck Pain — Typical Initial Care Plan

**Patient Profile:** 62-year-old male; cervical spondylosis (imaging: C5-C6 narrowing); baseline NRS 6/10; some radicular pain into right shoulder; PROMIS PF T-score 38; low-medium risk

**Phase Assignment:** Phase 1-2 (early transition)

**Pain Phenotype:** Mixed (nociceptive-dominant with neuropathic features; some arm pain from nerve involvement)

**Exercise Prescription:**

Phase 1 exercises (Weeks 1-6):
1. **Cervical ROM (active-assisted)** — 2 sets x 10 reps per direction, daily
   - Directions: Flexion, extension, rotation, lateral flexion (4 directions)
   - Cue: "Slow, controlled movement; stop before pain"

2. **Scapular retraction (seated, shoulder blade squeeze)** — 2 sets x 12 reps, daily
   - Cue: "Squeeze shoulder blades together, hold 3 seconds"

3. **Neck isometric holds** — 2 sets x 6-second holds, 3 directions (flexion, extension, lateral), daily
   - Cue: "Push head against hand; no movement; hold 6 seconds"

4. **Quadruped position holds (modified)** — 2 sets x 20-30 seconds, 3-4x/week
   - Cue: "Head neutral; engage shoulder stabilizers"
   - Modification: On knees if full plank too demanding

5. **Postural correction (hourly)** — Brief reminders
   - Cue: "Ears over shoulders, shoulders over hips"

6. **Aerobic conditioning** — 15-20 min walking, 3-4x/week
   - Note: Helps with neuropathic pain component

**Dosage:** NRS ≤2/10 during exercise; conservative (neuropathic component present)

**Coaching Cadence:**
- Biweekly check-in calls (20 min)
- Biweekly exercise coaching (form emphasis on neck stability)
- Monthly comprehensive review

**Education Plan:**
- Week 1-2: Condition-specific education on cervical spondylosis and ergonomics
- Week 3-4: Nerve-specific education ("nerve narrowing, but most spondylosis doesn't worsen with exercise")
- Week 5-6: PNE Module 1 (if pain persists despite exercise)

**PROM Targets:**
- NRS: 6 → 5 at 3 months, ≤3 at 6-12 months
- PROMIS PF: 38 → 40 at 3 months, 43-45 at 6-12 months

**Patient Goals:**
1. "Return to desktop work without neck stiffness" — Target 4+ hours by Month 6
2. "Golf without arm pain" — Target 9 holes by Month 6, 18 by Month 12

**Escalation Thresholds:**
- Progressive arm weakness (grip, hand function) → urgent PT/neurology evaluation
- New bowel/bladder symptoms → myelopathy protocol (red flag)

**Phase Progression Criteria (toward Phase 2 by Week 6-8):**
- NRS ≤5/10
- Arm pain (if present) stable or improving
- Neck ROM improved 10-15 degrees in at least 2 directions
- Postural awareness improving

### 4.5 Shoulder (Rotator Cuff / Impingement) — Typical Initial Care Plan

**Patient Profile:** 58-year-old female; right shoulder rotator cuff strain (no tear); baseline NRS 7/10; pain with overhead activities; PROMIS PF T-score 36; moderate risk (STarT Back 5)

**Phase Assignment:** Phase 1 (Foundation) — 8-10 week duration

**Pain Phenotype:** Nociceptive-dominant (tissue strain, impingement pain)

**Exercise Prescription:**

Phase 1 exercises (Weeks 1-10):
1. **Pendulum swings (gravity-assisted shoulder ROM)** — 2-3 sets x 30-second circles, 2x/day
   - Cue: "Small circles, let gravity do the work; no active muscle contraction"
   - Modification: Increase circle size as ROM improves

2. **Supine internal rotation (IR) with table assistance** — 2 sets x 8 reps, 1x/day
   - Cue: "Use other arm to assist; no pain into shoulder"
   - Progression: Progress to gravity-assisted (side-lying)

3. **Prone (or standing) scapular retraction (shoulder blade squeeze)** — 2 sets x 12 reps, daily
   - Cue: "Squeeze shoulder blades back; hold 3 seconds"
   - Rationale: Scapular stability prevents impingement

4. **Isometric internal rotation (side-lying)** — 2 sets x 6 reps x 5-second holds, daily
   - Cue: "Push elbow into body; no movement; hold"

5. **Supine shoulder shrugs (scapular elevation)** — 2 sets x 12 reps, daily
   - Cue: "Lift shoulders up toward ears, hold 2 seconds, lower"

6. **Cervical and shoulder stretches** — 30-second holds, 2-3x daily
   - Avoid aggressive stretching (shoulder impingement)

**Dosage:** NRS ≤2/10 during; conservative progression

**Coaching Cadence:**
- Biweekly check-in calls (20 min)
- Biweekly exercise coaching (critical for form; prevent re-injury)
- Monthly comprehensive review

**Education Plan:**
- Week 1-2: Condition-specific education ("rotator cuff strain, healing with exercise")
- Week 3-4: Activity modification education (avoid overhead until Phase 2)
- Week 6-8: PNE Module 1 (if pain persists beyond expected timeline)

**PROM Targets:**
- NRS: 7 → 6 at 3 months, ≤4 at 6 months, ≤2 at 12 months
- PROMIS PF: 36 → 38 at 3 months, 40-42 at 6 months, ≥45 at 12 months
- Shoulder ROM: Target near-normal by Month 6 (internal rotation, external rotation, forward flex)

**Patient Goals:**
1. "Return to overhead sports (tennis, swimming)" — Phased approach; Phase 3+ (Month 8+)
2. "Perform household tasks (dishes, cooking) without pain" — Target by Month 4

**Escalation Thresholds:**
- NRS >8/10 with significant weakness → imaging review (rule out rotator cuff tear)
- No improvement in ROM by Week 4 → PT reassessment
- Recurrent impingement pain episodes → consider corticosteroid injection prior to Phase 2 progression

**Phase Progression Criteria (toward Phase 2 by Week 10):**
- NRS ≤5/10
- Shoulder ROM improved: External rotation >0 degrees, internal rotation >90 degrees
- Pain-free overhead reaching to shoulder height
- Exercise compliance good
- Scapular stability improving (visible in form)

### 4.8 Multi-Site / Nociplastic-Dominant — Typical Initial Care Plan

**Patient Profile:** 61-year-old female; widespread pain (neck, shoulders, low back, knees); fibromyalgia-like presentation; baseline NRS 7/10; PROMIS PF T-score 29; high catastrophizing (PCS 28), depression screen positive (PHQ-2 = 2); HIGH RISK

**Phase Assignment:** Phase 1 (Foundation) — 12-16 week duration (extended due to complexity and psychosocial risk)

**Pain Phenotype:** Nociplastic-dominant (central sensitization, widespread pain, sleep disturbance, fatigue)

**Exercise Prescription (Aerobic Emphasis):**

Phase 1 exercises (Weeks 1-16):
1. **Walking (treadmill preferred for safety)** — Start 10-15 min, daily if tolerated
   - Progressive: Increase by 2-3 min weekly
   - Target by Week 12: 25-30 min, 5-6x/week
   - Pace: Comfortable conversation pace; HR <70% of age-predicted max
   - Rationale: Aerobic exercise prioritized for nociplastic pain

2. **Swimming or water aerobics** — 15-20 min, 2-3x/week (if available)
   - Low-impact, thermally comfortable
   - Alternative: pool walking

3. **Stationary cycling** — 10-15 min, 2-3x/week
   - Light resistance; steady-state effort

4. **Gentle yoga or tai chi (modified)** — 15-20 min, 2-3x/week
   - Emphasis: Mind-body awareness, breathing, gentle movement
   - Avoid hot yoga or intense power yoga

5. **Basic strengthening (very light)** — 2 sets x 8 reps per exercise, 2-3x/week
   - Single leg stands, glute bridges, quadriceps sets, shoulder shrugs
   - LIGHT load; focus on consistency not intensity

**Dosage Principle:** TIME-CONTINGENT, not pain-contingent
- Exercise regardless of pain; pain does NOT mean harm
- Goal: "Do this many minutes even if pain is present"
- Pain response: Expect NRS 3-5/10 during exercise; ACCEPT this; should return to baseline within 2-3 hours
- Avoid "pain flare" excuse to skip; walking "through" pain is therapeutic

**Coaching Cadence (High-Intensity):**
- Weekly check-in calls (20 min) — Tuesdays 10 AM
- Biweekly exercise coaching (live video), plus weekly exercise reminder call
- Weekly educational module session (structured CBT/PNE)
- Comprehensive review at Month 2, 4, 6 (60 min each)

**Education Plan (PNE + CBT Emphasis):**

**Weeks 1-2: Pain Neuroscience Education (Module 1)**
- Topic: "How Pain Works — The Nervous System Perspective"
- Format: 20-minute video + 3-page handout
- Content: Central sensitization, pain amplification, nervous system upregulation; pain ≠ tissue damage
- Goal: Normalize widespread pain; reduce catastrophizing narrative

**Week 2-3: CBT Module 4 — Unhelpful Thinking Patterns**
- Topic: "Catastrophizing and Pain Thoughts"
- Format: 25-minute video + worksheet
- Content: Identify catastrophic thoughts; reframe ("My pain is widespread, but my body is safe")
- Goal: Directly target high PCS score

**Week 3-4: CBT Module 2 — Relaxation and Breathing Techniques**
- Topic: "Reducing Physical Tension Through Breathwork"
- Format: 2x 15-minute guided audio (progressive muscle relaxation)
- Frequency: Practice 1x/day (at bedtime or midday)
- Goal: Reduce hypervigilance; improve sleep (depression component)

**Week 4-5: CBT Module 5 — Graded Exposure to Fear-Inducing Activities**
- Topic: "Returning to Activities Despite Pain"
- Content: Build exposure hierarchy (e.g., "I'm afraid to walk because it hurts, but pain ≠ harm")
- Frequency: Discuss at each weekly coaching call; use walking as exposure tool

**Week 6-8: PNE Module 2 — Neuroplasticity and Pain Recovery**
- Topic: "Your Nervous System Can Recover"
- Format: 20-minute video
- Goal: Hope-building; reinforce that central sensitization is reversible

**Month 3: CBT Module 3 — Activity Pacing**
- Topic: "Pacing and Activity Quotas"
- Content: Avoid boom-bust cycles ("push hard when better, crash when pain spikes")
- Goal: Sustainable activity increase

**Month 4: Sleep Module (if not already done)**
- Topic: "Sleep and Chronic Pain"
- Note: Depression + fibromyalgia often involves sleep disruption

**Months 5-12: Consolidation and Reinforcement**
- Monthly module review with care coordinator
- Patient increasingly independent with PNE concepts
- Advance to more complex topics (stress, identity, return-to-meaning)

**PROM Targets (Realistic for Nociplastic Pain):**
- NRS: 7 → 5-6 at 3 months (modest improvement expected initially), ≤4 at 6-12 months
  - Note: May not reach NRS <3; goal is functional improvement more than pain elimination
- PROMIS Physical Function: 29 → 32 at 3 months, 35-38 at 6-12 months
- PROMIS Pain Interference: Baseline → Reduce by 5-8 T-points over 6 months
- PROMIS Depression (if captured): Reduce by 3-5 T-points (PHQ-9 formal screen important)
- PROMIS Sleep (if available): Target improvement by Month 4
- PCS: 28 → 22 at 3 months, <15 at 12 months (catastrophizing reduction critical for prognosis)

**Patient Goals (SMART):**
1. "Walk 30 minutes without stopping, pain acceptable"
   - 3-month: Walk 20 min, NRS 4-5/10 acceptable (mindful that pain ≠ harm)
   - 6-month: Walk 30 min consistently
   - 12-month: Walk 45+ min; pain irrelevant to continuing

2. "Return to light gardening 1-2 hours/week"
   - 3-month: Tolerate light activity 20 min
   - 6-month: Tolerate 1 hour with one rest break
   - 12-month: Gardening 1-2 hours without significant impact on next day

3. "Improve sleep quality"
   - 3-month: Sleep 6+ hours/night most nights (from baseline ~5 hours)
   - 6-month: Sleep 7+ hours most nights, fewer middle-night awakenings
   - 12-month: Consistent sleep without pain-related interruptions

**Mental Health Escalation / Coordination:**
- PHQ-2 positive at baseline → MANDATORY referral to mental health provider (psychology or psychiatry)
- Timeline: Referral within 1 week of enrollment
- Ongoing: Monthly depression screening; if PHQ-9 worsens, adjust referral (increase frequency, consider medication)
- MSK care and behavioral health run in parallel

**Escalation Thresholds (Specific to Nociplastic):**
- NRS increase >3 points without new activity change → Escalation: Review catastrophizing, increase PNE frequency
- PHQ-2 score increases or suicidal ideation → Mental health crisis protocol
- No functional improvement (PROMIS PF) by Month 4 despite good compliance → Reassess: Is patient still in "pain-contingent" thinking? Escalate PNE intensity
- Appetite or energy decline → Medical evaluation (rule out medical cause; depression)
- Sleep worsening despite education → Consider sleep medicine referral

**Phase Progression Criteria (toward Phase 2 by Week 16, Month 4):**
- Walking consistently 25-30 min; pain present but not limiting participation
- NRS stable or improving trend (≤6/10 average)
- PROMIS PF improvement ≥3 T-points
- PCS decreased by ≥5 points (catastrophizing improving)
- PHQ-9 stable or improving (with mental health support)
- Sleep improving (reported 6+ hours most nights)
- Exercise compliance ≥80% (not required to be pain-free to participate)
- Patient increasingly using PNE language ("pain is safe," "nervous system is learning")

**Phase 2 Progression (Weeks 17-32, Months 5-8):**
- Continue aerobic emphasis; add light strengthening as tolerated
- Continue weekly PNE reinforcement
- CBT consolidation; patient applying tools independently
- Gradual increase in duration/intensity of activities (NOT intensity of exercise load, but duration of walking, gardening, etc.)

**Long-Term Prognosis Note:**
Nociplastic pain recovers more slowly. Realistic 12-month trajectory:
- NRS: High starting point (7) → Moderate improvement (4-5)
- Function: Significant improvement in meaningful activities
- Patient perception: "Pain still present, but doesn't control my life"
- Goal shifts from pain elimination to functional engagement and meaning

## 7. Care Plan Review and Modification Triggers

### 7.1 Scheduled Reviews

**Monthly PROM Reassessment:**
- NRS pain score
- Functional measure (PROMIS or condition-specific)
- Brief check-in question: "How is your pain this month compared to last?"

**Quarterly Comprehensive Review (Months 3, 6, 9, 12):**
- Full PROM battery re-administration
- Pain phenotype re-evaluation (has it shifted?)
- Risk stratification re-score (STarT Back or Keele)
- Psychosocial screening (PHQ-2, GAD-2, PCS, TSK as applicable)
- Exercise tolerance and form assessment
- Goal progress review
- Phase transition readiness assessment
- Care plan modifications documented

### 7.2 Unscheduled Modifications

**Trigger: Flare-Up or Pain Increase**
- If NRS increases >2 points for >1 week → Review exercise load; may reduce phase or extend current phase
- If flare-up, often temporary; reassure patient and modify briefly, then resume

**Trigger: New Symptoms**
- New neurological symptoms (numbness, weakness) → Red flag protocol (see KB_Red_Flag_Screening.md)
- New pain location → Re-evaluate pain phenotype; may require additional assessment

**Trigger: Patient Request**
- Patient may request modification at any time
- Document request and rationale; update plan collaboratively

**Trigger: Significant Psychosocial Event**
- Patient reports depression, anxiety, or life stressor → May increase coaching frequency; consider psychology referral
- PHQ-2 ≥2 or GAD-2 ≥2 → Mental health referral escalation

### 7.3 Phase Transition Criteria

**Transition from Phase 1 to Phase 2:**
Criteria (ALL must be met):
- NRS reduced by ≥1 point from baseline (e.g., 8 → 7) OR Pain stable and functional improvement clear
- PROMIS PF improved ≥2 T-points from baseline
- Patient performing all Phase 1 exercises with good form at pain ≤2/10
- Exercise compliance ≥75% (5+ days/week for 3-4 week period)
- Patient reports readiness to advance ("I feel ready to progress")
- No new red flags or escalation needs

Typical timing: Week 6-8 for Phase 1 (may extend if criteria not met)

**Transition from Phase 2 to Phase 3:**
Criteria:
- NRS ≤5/10, stable or improving trend
- PROMIS PF improved ≥4 T-points from baseline
- Patient tolerating Phase 2 exercises at pain ≤1/10
- Exercise compliance excellent (5+ days/week)
- Functional improvements visible (e.g., walking longer, climbing stairs easier)
- Patient-reported goals showing progress

Typical timing: Month 3-4 (if Phase 1 lasted 8-10 weeks)

**Transition from Phase 3 to Phase 4:**
Criteria:
- NRS <3/10, pain minimal
- PROMIS PF at or near baseline normal (T-score ≥48)
- Patient independent with Phase 3 exercises; form excellent
- Patient ready for maintenance and self-management
- Return-to-activity goals achieved or nearly achieved

Typical timing: Month 8-10

### 7.4 When to Modify vs. When to Escalate

**MODIFY the care plan:**
- Exercise is too easy or too hard (adjust load, reps, frequency)
- Patient requesting change in coaching cadence (e.g., "Can we do this by email instead of phone?")
- Patient making good progress but wants to focus on different goal
- Seasonal activity changes (e.g., shift from indoor walking to outdoor hiking)
- Minor pain flare (temporary reduction in load)

**ESCALATE (refer out):**
- New red flag symptoms or concerning findings (see KB_Red_Flag_Screening.md)
- Mental health deterioration (depression, anxiety, suicidal ideation)
- NRS >8/10 despite 8+ weeks of appropriate care
- No functional improvement by Month 4
- Recurrent escalation triggers despite modifications
- Patient requesting specialist referral
- Imaging or advanced diagnostics needed
- Medication management outside KIMI scope (new opioids, psychiatric meds)

## 8. Evidence Base

### 8.1 Key Clinical Guidelines and Evidence

**Osteoarthritis (OA) Knee, Hip:**
- OARSI 2019 Guidelines for OA Management: Recommend exercise and activity as first-line treatment; support staged progression
- NICE Guidelines on OA: Recommend 2-3 sessions/week aerobic and resistance exercise
- Cochrane Reviews on Exercise for OA: Strong evidence for pain and function improvement
- Evidence: Effect size moderate to large (0.5-1.0 for PROM improvements over 6-12 months)

**Chronic Low Back Pain (LBP):**
- NICE Guidelines on LBP: Recommend exercise, cognitive behavioral approaches, psychosocial assessment
- JOSPT (Journal of Orthopedic & Sports Physical Therapy) CPG on LBP: Stages care based on pain and risk; recommend early cognitive behavioral intervention for high-risk patients
- Evidence: STarT Back matched care reduces pain and disability compared to standard care

**Neck Pain / Cervical Spondylosis:**
- NICE Guidelines: Recommend exercise, postural education, activity grading
- Evidence: ROM and strengthening exercises show benefit; avoid aggressive mobilization in presence of radiculopathy

**Rotator Cuff / Shoulder:**
- JOSPT CPG on Shoulder Impingement: Recommend staged ROM → strengthening → sport-specific progression
- Evidence: Exercise programs show 70-80% improvement in rotator cuff pain over 8-12 weeks

**Fibromyalgia / Nociplastic Pain:**
- EULAR Guidelines on Fibromyalgia: Recommend aerobic exercise, cognitive behavioral therapy, education
- Evidence: Aerobic exercise (25-35 min, 3-5x/week) and multimodal cognitive behavioral approach show best outcomes
- Central sensitization upregulation best addressed by time-contingent (not pain-contingent) exercise and pain neuroscience education

### 8.2 Pain Phenotyping Evidence

**Nociceptive Pain Classification (Tissue Damage):**
- Evidence-based: Localized pain correlates to movement testing; improves with loading and healing time
- Management: Progressive tissue loading, ROM restoration

**Neuropathic Pain Classification (Nerve Involvement):**
- Evidence-based: Radiating pain, dermatomal pattern, nerve provocation tests positive
- Management: Neural mobilization, aerobic conditioning, avoid nerve provocation

**Nociplastic Pain Classification (Central Sensitization):**
- Evidence-based: Widespread pain, normal imaging, amplified pain response, sleep disturbance
- Management: Aerobic exercise, pain neuroscience education, cognitive behavioral therapy, time-contingent activity

### 8.3 PROM Selection and MCID Evidence

**Numeric Rating Scale (NRS):**
- MCID: 2-3 point reduction (published in multiple trials)
- Use: Universal pain measure; easy for all patients

**PROMIS Physical Function:**
- MCID: 2-4 T-points (depends on baseline; typically 3-4 points)
- Use: Standardized functional measure; comparable across conditions
- Advantage: Calibrated to U.S. population; precise T-scores

**PROMIS Pain Interference:**
- MCID: 5-point reduction
- Use: Captures how pain affects daily activities

**Condition-Specific Measures (ODI, LEFS, etc.):**
- ODI (Oswestry Disability Index) MCID: 8-10 points
- LEFS (Lower Extremity Functional Scale) MCID: 9-12 points
- Use: Condition-targeted sensitivity

### 8.4 CBT and Pain Neuroscience Education Evidence

**Pain Neuroscience Education (PNE):**
- Evidence: RCTs show PNE reduces catastrophizing, kinesiophobia, and pain when combined with exercise
- Effect: Moderate effect sizes (d = 0.5-0.8) on pain and function
- Mechanism: Reduces fear-avoidance, normalizes central sensitization understanding
- References: Moseley & Butler "Explain Pain" framework; systematic reviews support multimodal PNE + exercise

**Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT):**
- Evidence: Strong evidence for CBT in chronic pain (meta-analyses show moderate to large effect sizes)
- Specific modules: Cognitive restructuring (addressing catastrophizing), exposure therapy (kinesiophobia), relaxation/breathing (anxiety)
- Effect: Most benefit when integrated with exercise (CBT alone less effective than combination)

### 8.5 Risk Stratification Evidence

**STarT Back Screening Tool:**
- Evidence: Matched care based on STarT Back risk level reduces healthcare costs and improves outcomes
- Categories: Low-risk (minimal intervention), medium-risk (mixed), high-risk (multimodal)
- Reference: Validated in UK, Australia, other health systems; incorporated into NICE guidelines

**Keele STarT MSK:**
- Evidence: Extension of STarT Back to multi-site MSK conditions
- Identifies: Patients likely to have poor prognosis without intervention
- Prognostic value: High-risk patients 3-4x more likely to have poor outcomes

### 8.6 Exercise Progression Evidence

**Phase-Based Progression:**
- Evidence: Progressive loading from ROM → strength → power/sport is evidence-based (supported by JOSPT CPGs)
- Mechanism: Allows tissue adaptation; reduces re-injury risk
- Timing: Typical 6-8 week phases, but individualized based on PROM and pain response

**Exercise Dosage:**
- Frequency: 3-5 days/week optimal for most MSK conditions (supported by meta-analyses)
- Duration: 20-40 min sessions typical
- Intensity: Progressed from light to moderate based on phase and pain phenotype
