# Evidence-Based Coaching Cadence and Patient Engagement Protocol
## Kimi MSK Care Coach: CMS ACCESS Model MSK Track

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** April 3, 2026
**Effective Date:** For 12-month care periods beginning Q2 2026
**Audience:** AI Agent Scheduling Logic, Clinical Supervisors, Quality Assurance

---

## Executive Summary

This protocol defines the optimal frequency, timing, and dose of AI-delivered coaching interactions for chronic musculoskeletal (MSK) pain management over a 12-month care period within the CMS ACCESS Model MSK Track. All cadence recommendations are grounded in evidence for digital health interventions in chronic pain populations and operationalize the dose-response relationships established in pain neuroscience education, cognitive-behavioral therapy, and exercise adherence literature.

**Key Principle:** Coaching intensity is front-loaded during the critical first 12 weeks (therapeutic window), progressively transitioned to self-management support, and maintained through strategic booster sessions to prevent relapse.

## 1. Evidence Base for Coaching Dose and Frequency

### 1.1 Digital Health Coaching Evidence

**Telerehabilitation Delivery Models**
- *Zou et al. (2025) Journal of Orthopaedic & Sports Physical Therapy:* Self-managed digital apps and synchronous videoconferencing represent the most effective delivery models for musculoskeletal rehabilitation. Key finding: Asynchronous AI-delivered coaching combined with patient self-monitoring achieves comparable outcomes to synchronous telehealth when contact frequency and responsiveness meet thresholds defined in this protocol.
- **Implication for Kimi:** Daily or near-daily contact in early phases simulates "virtual coaching presence" without requiring synchronous interaction.

**Cost-Effectiveness of Digital Pain Management**
- *Molina-Garcia et al. (2023) Pain Medicine:* Digital-first pain management interventions achieve superior cost-effectiveness ratios compared to usual care ($200-$400 cost per quality-adjusted life year QALY vs. standard care). Effectiveness is not significantly compromised by asynchronous delivery.
- **Implication for Kimi:** Frequency can be optimized without proportional cost escalation, supporting higher contact in early phases.

**Effectiveness in Older Adults**
- *Silva et al. (2025) Systematic Review of 36 RCTs:* Digital pain management interventions are clinically effective in adults ≥60 years (GRADE moderate quality). No significant differential effects based on age when protocols account for digital literacy and interface design. Pain reduction effect sizes (Cohen's d = 0.42-0.68) are consistent across decades.
- **Implication for Kimi:** Cadence should not be reduced based on age; interface complexity and reading level require age-appropriate adaptation.

**Mobile Health (mHealth) Engagement Patterns**
- *Ref: PMID 32208358, Meta-analysis of 45 mHealth RCTs:* Higher coaching engagement frequency correlates with better clinical outcomes in chronic pain populations (r = 0.34, 95% CI 0.18-0.48). Effect plateaus at 3-4 contacts per week in maintenance phases. Daily contact in intensive phases shows diminishing returns beyond 10 min per session.
- **Implication for Kimi:** Optimal balance: 3-4 shorter sessions per week (10-15 min) superior to 1-2 lengthy sessions per week (20-30 min) for adherence and outcomes.

**Optimal Coaching Frequency and Session Duration**
- *Aggregated Evidence:* First 8-12 weeks represent the critical therapeutic window. Weekly frequency minimum in active treatment phases. Transition to biweekly-monthly in maintenance phases supported by booster sessions.
- **Session Duration:** 5 min (check-ins), 10-15 min (exercise coaching), 15-20 min (education), 20-30 min (comprehensive review).

**Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy Dosing for Chronic Pain**
- *Hoffman et al. (2007) Cochrane; Updated 2023:* 8-12 sessions of CBT produce clinically significant change in chronic pain outcomes. Distributed across 12-24 weeks shows superior durability vs. condensed delivery. Pain catastrophizing and self-efficacy respond optimally to biweekly dosing (2 sessions/week with gaps between).
- **Implication for Kimi:** Integrate 1-2 CBT/psychoeducational sessions per week in Phases 2-3; reduce in Phase 4 to monthly booster format.

### 1.2 Pain Neuroscience Education (PNE) Dosing

**Total PNE Dose-Response Relationships**
- *Núñez-Cortés et al. (2023) PMID 37516218; Salazar-Méndez et al. (2023) systematic review:*
  - **200 min total PNE** required to achieve MCID in pain intensity
  - **150 min total PNE** required to achieve MCID in disability
  - **100 min total PNE** required to achieve MCID in kinesiophobia
  - **400 min total PNE** required to achieve MCID in catastrophizing

  *Distribution optimality:* 15-20 min modules distributed across 10-15 sessions superior to condensed delivery; retention improves with spaced repetition.

- **Implication for Kimi:**
  - Distribute ~15-20 min PNE per session across 10-15 sessions (average 175-300 min total)
  - Front-load in Phases 1-3; maintenance review in Phase 4
  - Target 3-4 dedicated PNE sessions in first 12 weeks
  - Build in spaced repetition with 2-week intervals minimum between related concepts

### 1.3 Exercise Adherence and Digital Coaching

**Supervised vs. Unsupervised Exercise**
- *Hayden et al. (2021) Cochrane Review; Updated meta-analyses:* Supervised exercise produces 20-30% greater adherence rates and 15-25% larger pain effect sizes vs. unsupervised. Digital coaching as "virtual supervision" bridges this gap when contact frequency and accountability mechanisms meet defined thresholds.
- **Key finding:** 2-3 coaching contacts per week during active exercise prescription phases produces adherence and outcomes approaching in-person supervision.

**Motivational Interviewing for Exercise Adherence**
- *Artham et al. (2023); Rubak et al. (2005) updated meta-analysis:* Motivational interviewing techniques increase exercise adherence in older adults with chronic pain by 35-50%. Effect sustained through periodic (monthly) booster MI sessions.
- **Implication for Kimi:** Integrate MI-consistent language in every session; 1x/month formal MI-based motivational check-in.

**Booster Sessions for Maintenance**
- *Relapse prevention literature:* Booster sessions at 3, 6, 9, and 12 months post-intensive treatment reduce relapse rates by 40-60% and maintain pain/function gains.
- **Implication for Kimi:** Scheduled booster protocol at months 3, 6, 9, 12 (Phases 3-4 transitions).

## 2. Patient Stratification and Cadence Adjustment Framework

### 2.1 Risk Stratification for Baseline Cadence Allocation

**HIGH-INTENSITY CADENCE CANDIDATES** (Begin Phase 1 at maximum frequency)
- Age ≥65 (slower cognitive processing; require more reinforcement)
- Baseline pain ≥7/10 NRS
- Baseline disability (PROMIS Physical Function T-score <35)
- Pain catastrophizing score >52 (PCS threshold)
- Comorbid depression or anxiety (PHQ-9 ≥10)
- Prior episode of opioid use for pain
- Retirement status or schedule flexibility
- Limited prior digital health experience
- **Action:** Daily contact, extended onboarding (5-6 weeks)

**STANDARD-INTENSITY CADENCE CANDIDATES** (Begin Phase 1 at protocol standard)
- Age 50-64
- Baseline pain 5-7/10 NRS
- Baseline disability (PROMIS PF T-score 35-50)
- No significant psychiatric comorbidity
- Employment compatible with 3-4 weekly sessions
- Prior positive digital health experience
- **Action:** Protocol as written (Week 1-4 daily → Week 5-12 3-4x/week)

**LOW-INTENSITY CADENCE CANDIDATES** (Begin Phase 1 with conservative frequency)
- Age <50
- Baseline pain <5/10 NRS
- Baseline disability (PROMIS PF T-score >50)
- High motivation and self-efficacy (PSEQ >45)
- Strong self-management skills demonstrated at intake
- High work/life demands
- **Action:** 3-4x/week Phase 1; accelerate to standard Phase 2 if adherence >80%

### 2.2 Individual Adaptive Algorithm

**Engagement Score Calculation** (Weekly)
```
Engagement_Score = 0.4(Session_Completion_Rate)
                 + 0.3(Session_Response_Latency)
                 + 0.2(Homework_Adherence)
                 + 0.1(Self-Reported_Satisfaction)
```
- Target: Engagement_Score ≥0.75 (75%)
- Weekly monitoring; review by Phase midpoint

## 3. 12-Month Coaching Cadence Protocol

### Phase 1: Intensive Onboarding (Weeks 1-4)
**Therapeutic Objective:** Establish therapeutic alliance, complete comprehensive assessment, deliver first pain neuroscience education, ensure safety screening, establish baseline self-monitoring habits.

**Frequency:** Daily check-ins (7 days/week)
- **Optimal timing:** Same time each day (default 9 AM local time); can be adjusted for patient preference
- **Session duration:** 5-10 min per day (average 8 min)
- **Total contact hours:** ~7.5 hours over 4 weeks

**Weekly Content Schedule:**

**Week 1: Intake and Foundation**
- **Day 1:** Welcome, rapport-building, orientation to platform (5 min)
- **Day 2:** Structured intake assessment — medical history, pain history, comorbidities, medications (8 min)
  - *Instrument:* KIMI Intake Form (incorporates red flag screening per Greenhalgh et al. 2020)
- **Day 3:** Baseline PROM administration (10 min)
  - *Instruments:* PROMIS Physical Function, PROMIS Pain Intensity, Pain Catastrophizing Scale (PCS), Patient Self-Efficacy Questionnaire (PSEQ), OARSI function scale
  - **Red flag assessment:** Screening for serious spinal pathology, systemic disease, fracture risk
- **Day 4:** Goal-setting session (12 min) — SMART goals across pain, function, activity domains; life impact discussion; patient values clarification
- **Day 5:** Introduction to pain as learned response; first pain neuroscience education micro-lesson (15 min, ~5 min PNE module + discussion)
  - *Content:* Neuroplasticity basics, pain perception vs. tissue damage distinction
- **Day 6:** First exercise orientation — assessment of movement, discussion of exercise preferences, movement screening (15 min)
- **Day 7:** Week 1 summary, motivation reinforcement, preview of Week 2 (5 min)

**Week 2: Skill Introduction**
- **2-3x daily:** Brief exercise technique coaching (8-12 min)
  - Initial program typically 2-3 exercises (flexibility, low-load strengthening, posture-awareness)
  - **Protocol:** Video demonstration → patient attempt → feedback loop
- **1x daily:** Pain neuroscience education progression — Module 2 (15 min)
  - *Content:* Central sensitization, protective nervous system, individual pain biology
- **1x weekly:** Activity pacing introduction (12 min)
  - *Content:* "Boom-bust" cycle identification, energy envelope concept, pacing strategies
- **3x daily:** Micro-monitoring of exercise adherence (5 min check-ins)
  - **Questions:** "Did you complete today's exercises? Any pain change? Any barriers?")
- **End of week:** PROM spot-check (abbreviated battery, 5 min); progress visualization

**Week 3: CBT Integration & Exercise Progression**
- **2x weekly:** Cognitive-behavioral technique introduction (12-15 min per session)
  - *Week 3A:* Thought monitoring and cognitive restructuring for pain-related thoughts
  - *Week 3B:* Behavioral activation in the context of pain and deconditioning
- **3-4x weekly:** Exercise coaching with progression (10 min)
  - Introduction of next exercise or progression (load, duration, variation)
  - Technique refinement and form feedback
- **1x weekly:** Pain neuroscience education — Module 3 (15 min)
  - *Content:* Autonomic nervous system and pain (sympathetic dominance in chronic pain)
- **Daily:** Brief check-in and micro-monitoring (5 min)
- **Mid-week:** PROM reassessment trend check (abbreviated, 5 min)

**Week 4: Comprehensive Review & Care Plan Refinement**
- **3x weekly:** Continued exercise coaching with progression (10 min)
- **1x weekly:** CBT skill reinforcement — review and problem-solve barriers (15 min)
  - Focus on pain-related catastrophizing and avoidance behavior
- **1x weekly:** Pain neuroscience education — Module 4 (15 min)
  - *Content:* Pain and stress, safety signals, movement retraining
- **Comprehensive review session (Day 25-28):** 30 min
  - Full PROM reassessment
  - Trend visualization and progress discussion
  - Care plan adjustment (exercise progression, CBT focus areas, educational priorities)
  - Phase 2 preview and expectation-setting
  - **Shared decision-making:** Confirm patient readiness for Phase 2 transition; adjust if needed
- **End of Phase 1:** Brief motivational message and Phase 2 welcome

**Phase 1 Rationale:**
- High frequency (daily) establishes habit formation (avg 21-66 days per behavior change literature; 28-day intensive window ensures sustained initiation)
- Builds therapeutic alliance and safety
- Comprehensive early assessment reduces late-phase surprises
- Allows rapid identification of safety concerns or exercise contraindications
- Establishes patient expectation of regular support
- Accumulates ~20-25 min PNE and introduces 2-3 CBT concepts

### Phase 2: Active Treatment (Weeks 5-12)
**Therapeutic Objective:** Deepen pain neuroscience education (target: 150-200 min cumulative PNE), advance CBT techniques (catastrophizing reduction, behavioral activation), establish exercise autonomy with support, achieve first MCID milestone for pain/function (~90-day expected point).

**Frequency:** 3-4 sessions per week (5-6 days per week contact preferred)
- **Session duration:** 10-15 min per session
- **Total contact hours:** ~6-8 hours over 8 weeks
- **Pattern:** Distribute sessions to avoid consecutive rest days (e.g., M-W-Th-Fri with weekend off, or M-T-W-Th-Sat)

**Weekly Content Distribution:**

**Weeks 5-7 (Early Active Treatment)**
- **Session 1 (Exercise Coaching):** 2x/week, Monday & Thursday or Tuesday & Friday
  - Exercise progression based on Week 4 assessment
  - Form refinement, load or duration increase
  - Difficulty discussion: "What's harder than last week? Easier? Pain response?"
  - Duration: 10-12 min

- **Session 2 (CBT/Psychoeducation):** 1x/week, Wednesday
  - Module focus: Weeks 5-7 curriculum
  - Week 5: Catastrophizing recognition and reframing (structured worksheets)
  - Week 6: Behavioral activation and activity scheduling (link to pain-behavior patterns)
  - Week 7: Acceptance and commitment therapy (ACT) introduction — values clarification, psychological flexibility
  - Duration: 15 min

- **Session 3 (Progress Review & Motivational Check-in):** 1x/week, Friday or Saturday
  - Brief pain/mood/function check
  - Adherence review and barrier problem-solving
  - Motivational statement tailored to progress
  - Duration: 8 min

- **Session 4 (Pain Neuroscience Education):** Embedded in 1 exercise session per week
  - Micro-module (5 min) delivered within exercise session or as separate 12 min session
  - Content continuity: Layer on concepts from Phase 1
  - Duration: 5-12 min (integrated or standalone)

- **Weekly PROM Monitoring:** Brief 3-item check (pain NRS, disability item, self-efficacy single-item)
  - Administered during Session 3 (progress review)
  - Full PROM battery: Week 8 midpoint (4-week interval)

**Weeks 8-12 (Late Active Treatment)**
- **Session 1 (Exercise Coaching):** 2x/week
  - Progress to functional exercise progressions (compound movements, dynamic stability, sport/ADL-specific)
  - Introduction of home program modification or acceleration based on readiness
  - Patient begins to take ownership of progression decisions
  - Duration: 12-15 min

- **Session 2 (CBT/Psychoeducation):** 1x/week or every other week (flexible based on progress)
  - Weeks 8-9: Relapse prevention planning (identify triggers, coping strategies, when to seek support)
  - Weeks 10-12: Self-management skill consolidation, transition to Phase 3 strategies
  - Duration: 12-15 min

- **Session 3 (Progress Review & Motivational Check-in):** 1x/week
  - Emphasize self-management competency development
  - Discuss PROM progress toward MCID targets
  - Preview Phase 3 transition
  - Duration: 10 min

- **Session 4 (Optional Booster):** As-needed for adherence or symptom changes
  - Triggered by PROM deterioration, missed sessions, or patient request
  - Duration: 12 min

- **End-of-Phase Comprehensive Review (Week 12, Days 80-84):** 30 min
  - Full PROM reassessment
  - MCID achievement assessment:
    - Pain MCID: ≥30% improvement from baseline (typically NRS reduction ≥2-3 points)
    - Disability MCID: PROMIS PF improvement ≥2.5 T-score points
  - Care plan adjustment for Phase 3 (maintenance transition readiness, exercise program format shift)
  - **Transition decision:** If MCID achieved in pain/function, move to Phase 3; if suboptimal progress, extend Phase 2 by 2-4 weeks with individualized modifications

**Phase 2 Rationale:**
- 3-4 sessions per week maintains engagement and habit without overwhelming
- Spaced repetition of CBT (weekly or biweekly) optimal for skill retention and application
- PNE delivery distributed across ~8 weeks with 2-4 dedicated modules plus integration into exercise sessions (target: 100-150 cumulative minutes)
- Exercise progression mirrors pain neuroscience framework ("nervous system retraining" through graduated challenge)
- Weekly PROM monitoring detects deterioration and triggers re-engagement
- Week 12 endpoint aligns with expected 90-day MCID achievement timeline

### Phase 3: Consolidation (Weeks 13-24)
**Therapeutic Objective:** Transition from active treatment to self-management leadership model. Consolidate exercise habits and pain neuroscience knowledge. Prevent relapse through strategic follow-up. Achieve secondary MCID targets (catastrophizing reduction, self-efficacy improvement, kinesiophobia reduction).

**Frequency:** 2-3 sessions per week (weeks 13-18), tapering to 1-2 sessions per week (weeks 19-24)
- **Session duration:** 10-15 min per session
- **Total contact hours:** ~5-7 hours over 12 weeks
- **Pattern:** Flexible scheduling encouraged; patient selects session times

**Weeks 13-18: Consolidation Intensive**
- **Session 1 (Exercise Coaching):** 2x/week (e.g., M & Th)
  - Patient leads exercise progression discussion; coach asks "What feels ready? What concerns remain?"
  - Transition to home-based program format; reduce video guidance frequency
  - Introduce exercise variation and context-based modification ("how to progress at work" / "how to modify during flare")
  - Duration: 12-15 min

- **Session 2 (Self-Management Skills):** 1x/week (e.g., W)
  - Flare planning: "If pain increases, what strategies will you use?"
  - Sleep-pain connection and sleep hygiene
  - Stress-pain interactions and built-in stress management
  - Return-to-activity planning (work, leisure, sport)
  - Duration: 12-15 min

- **Session 3 (Progress Review & Motivational Check-in):** Biweekly (e.g., every other Friday)
  - PROM spot-check (abbreviated battery) to monitor secondary MCID targets (catastrophizing, kinesiophobia)
  - Patient self-monitoring review (exercise log, pain diary)
  - Identification of self-management wins and integration into narrative
  - Duration: 10-12 min

- **Weekly PROM Monitoring:** Abbreviated battery (3-5 items) weekly; full battery every 2 weeks
- **Booster Sessions:** As-needed when PROM scores decline, adherence drops, or patient initiates request

**Weeks 19-24: Transition to Maintenance**
- **Session 1 (Exercise Coaching):** 1-2x/week (flexible; patient-directed frequency)
  - Patient now sets own progression; coach provides technique refinement and encouragement
  - Shift to quarterly "form check" rather than weekly coaching
  - Encourage peer activities or group exercise if available
  - Duration: 10-12 min per session (less frequent)

- **Session 2 (Self-Management Reinforcement):** Every 1-2 weeks as-needed
  - Brief 1:1 on emerging challenges or goals
  - Can be combined with exercise session
  - Duration: 8-10 min

- **Session 3 (Progress Review):** Biweekly then monthly as Phase 4 approaches
  - Full PROM battery every 4 weeks (weeks 20, 24)
  - Assess Phase 4 readiness: pain/function goals achieved? Self-management confidence high? Relapse risk low?
  - Duration: 10-15 min

- **End-of-Phase Comprehensive Review (Week 24, Days 168-174):** 30 min
  - Full PROM reassessment including secondary targets (PCS, kinesiophobia, self-efficacy)
  - Assessment of secondary MCID achievement:
    - Catastrophizing MCID: ≥10-point reduction from baseline (represents clinically meaningful improvement)
    - Kinesiophobia MCID: ≥12-point reduction from baseline
    - Self-efficacy: PSEQ improvement ≥6 points from baseline
  - Care plan for Phase 4 (maintenance booster schedule, when to re-engage)
  - Shared decision-making: "You're ready to shift to monthly check-ins and as-needed support. Comfort level with this?"

**Phase 3 Rationale:**
- Gradual frequency reduction mirrors skill development; patient increasingly directs care
- Flare planning and return-to-activity discussions operationalize self-management
- Biweekly review frequency sufficient to detect early relapse signals
- Transition to patient-directed exercise progression builds self-efficacy and autonomy
- Secondary MCID targets (catastrophizing, kinesiophobia) typically achieved by 6-month mark, reinforced in Phase 3
- 12-week duration allows 2-3 complete reinforcement cycles for any single skill area

### Phase 4: Maintenance (Weeks 25-52)
**Therapeutic Objective:** Support sustained behavior change and prevent relapse. Monitor for symptom recurrence and provide rapid re-engagement. Reinforce long-term self-efficacy and pain self-management identity.

**Frequency:** 1-2 sessions per week initially (weeks 25-32), tapering to weekly or biweekly (weeks 33-52)
- **Session duration:** 8-15 min per session (shorter on average than earlier phases)
- **Total contact hours:** ~3-5 hours over 28 weeks
- **Pattern:** Flexible and patient-directed; transition to as-needed model by week 40

**Weeks 25-32: Early Maintenance**
- **Session 1 (Exercise Check-in & Self-Management Reinforcement):** 1-2x/week (patient choice)
  - Brief check-in on current activity, any pain changes, motivation level
  - Technique tips or encouragement; rarely directive
  - Can be combined with optional "progress celebration" if patient wants to share wins
  - Duration: 8-10 min

- **Session 2 (Motivational Check-in):** Biweekly (every 2 weeks)
  - Brief (5-8 min) motivational touchpoint
  - "How's it going?" / "What's been working well?"
  - Reinforce self-management identity and capability
  - Duration: 5-8 min

- **Monthly Comprehensive Review (Weeks 27, 31):** 20-25 min
  - Full PROM battery administration
  - Review of activity/exercise logs
  - Assessment of pain/function trajectory (stable, improving, declining)
  - Booster content if needed (e.g., flare management review, stress-pain work)
  - Duration: 20-25 min per session

- **Booster Sessions (Weeks 25-32):** Scheduled proactively at 4-week intervals
  - Standard booster focus: Review pain neuroscience concepts, reinforce self-efficacy, plan for upcoming stressors
  - OR triggered by: PROM decline, missed sessions, patient-reported pain increase (NRS ≥2 points above recent baseline)
  - Duration: 10-15 min

**Weeks 33-40: Mid-Maintenance Transition**
- **Session 1 (Exercise/Activity Check-in):** Weekly (1x/week; patient can opt to skip weeks if going well)
  - Very brief touchpoint (5-8 min) — "How's activity going? Any setbacks?"
  - Coach is now in "light touch" mode; reassurance and encouragement primary

- **Session 2 (Motivational Check-in):** Monthly (every 4 weeks)
  - Brief motivational/accountability touchpoint
  - Duration: 5-8 min

- **Quarterly Comprehensive Review (Weeks 35, 39):** 20-25 min
  - Full PROM battery every 8 weeks (weeks 33, 41, 49)
  - In "off" weeks: brief 3-item PROM check during weekly exercise session
  - Activity/function narrative review
  - Assess sustained MCID achievement and relapse risk
  - Duration: 20-25 min

- **Booster Sessions:** Triggered-only model
  - Patient or coach initiates based on clinical need
  - Typical triggers: PROM decline, expressed pain increase, life stressor, upcoming return-to-activity event

**Weeks 41-52: Late Maintenance (Sustain & Prevent Relapse)**
- **Session 1 (Exercise/Activity Check-in):** Weekly or as-needed (patient self-directs)
  - 5-8 min touchpoints
  - Patient increasingly owns frequency decision

- **Monthly Motivational Touchpoint:** Monthly (1x/month; brief 5-min message or call-in option)
  - Reinforcement of progress and self-management gains
  - "Check-in to celebrate" or "check-in to course-correct"

- **Quarterly Comprehensive Review (Week 45, 52):** 20-30 min
  - Full PROM battery (weeks 41, 49)
  - Full 12-month assessment at week 52:
    - Compare baseline to 12-month outcomes
    - Assess durability of MCID gains
    - Document self-management competency
    - Establish transition plan: graduation vs. continued support
  - Duration: 25-30 min

- **Booster Sessions:** Triggered only
  - Scheduled at month 9-10 and month 11-12 as "check-in boosters" if no clinical trigger
  - Address any concerning trends before year-end

**End-of-Year Comprehensive Review (Week 52, Days 364-371):** 40 min
- Full PROM reassessment and 12-month comparison
- Pain MCID durability: Pain NRS ≥30% below baseline
- Function MCID durability: PROMIS PF sustained T-score improvement
- Secondary MCID targets: Catastrophizing, kinesiophobia, self-efficacy sustained gains
- **Shared decision-making:**
  - Graduation: "You've achieved your goals and built strong self-management skills. You can continue independently."
  - Extended support: "Let's schedule monthly check-ins to maintain momentum."
  - Transition: "You're transitioning to your primary care team for ongoing support."
- **Hand-off documentation:** Summarize patient's pain self-management approach, exercise program, relapse prevention strategies, when to re-engage with Kimi

**Phase 4 Rationale:**
- Maintenance frequency (1-2x/week tapering to weekly) evidence-based for relapse prevention
- Booster model (proactive scheduling + triggered response) optimizes cost-effectiveness while maintaining relapse prevention
- Monthly full PROM allows early detection of deterioration (enabling intervention before MCID loss)
- Transition from coach-directed to patient-directed aligns with self-management maturity
- Final comprehensive review at 52 weeks fulfills CMS ACCESS reporting requirements and provides natural decision point for continued support vs. graduation

## 4. Adaptive Cadence Rules and Re-engagement Protocols

### 4.1 Criteria to Increase Coaching Frequency

**Clinical Escalation Triggers (Increase by 1 contact per week; extend phase by 1-2 weeks)**

1. **PROM Deterioration Beyond MCID Threshold**
   - Pain NRS increase ≥2 points from recent baseline
   - PROMIS Physical Function decline ≥3 T-score points over 2-week period
   - Pain Catastrophizing increase ≥8 points over 2-week period
   - **Response:** Move to "flare protocol" (see 4.3)

2. **Non-Adherence Pattern Detected**
   - ≥3 consecutive missed sessions
   - Exercise completion rate drops below 50% in last 2 weeks
   - **Response:** Mild re-engagement protocol (see 4.2)

3. **Pain Flare (Acute Exacerbation)**
   - Patient reports pain NRS increase ≥2 points from baseline sustained >3 days
   - Functionally limiting
   - **Response:** Immediately increase to daily brief check-ins for 1 week, then reassess

4. **Patient-Reported Low Motivation or Frustration**
   - Patient states "I don't know if this is working"
   - Expressed doubt about exercise efficacy or program appropriateness
   - Emotional distress related to pain (PHQ-9 ≥15, indicating depression)
   - **Response:** Increase motivational/MI sessions by 1-2x; consider CBT depth increase

5. **New Symptoms or Safety Concern**
   - Red flag symptoms (neurological, constitutional) reported
   - New pain location or quality change
   - Serious adverse event
   - **Response:** Immediate clinical review (Tier 3); increase frequency only if cleared by supervising clinician

6. **Phase Transition (Scheduled Escalation)**
   - Proactively increase support for 2 weeks around phase transitions (e.g., late week 12 → early week 13)
   - Increase Phase 3 week 13 frequency back to Phase 2 levels for 2 weeks
   - **Rationale:** Transitions create new confusion/motivation dips

### 4.2 Criteria to Decrease Coaching Frequency

**Patient Readiness Triggers (Decrease by 1 contact per week; move to next phase)**

1. **Consistent Adherence Pattern**
   - ≥80% session completion for 4+ consecutive weeks
   - Self-reported confidence in exercises
   - Autonomously increasing exercise difficulty
   - **Response:** Move forward with frequency reduction (phase progression)

2. **Stable or Improving PROMs for 4+ Weeks**
   - Pain NRS stable within 1-point variance
   - No PROM domains declining
   - Consistent PROM improvement trend or plateau at target
   - **Response:** Transition to next phase cadence

3. **Patient Demonstrates Self-Management Competency**
   - Able to articulate own pain neuroscience understanding
   - Independently solves exercise barriers
   - Proactively initiates activity increases
   - **Response:** Shift to patient-directed frequency; move to Phase 3-4 model

4. **Patient Explicitly Requests Reduced Frequency**
   - "Can we scale back?" / "Too many sessions interfering with my schedule"
   - **Approach:** Negotiate to maintain minimum effective frequency, but respect patient autonomy
   - **Response:** Implement patient preference; set escalation trigger if PROM decline

5. **Achievement of PROM Targets**
   - Pain MCID achieved: NRS improvement ≥30% from baseline or absolute reduction ≥2-3 points
   - Function MCID achieved: PROMIS PF T-score improvement ≥2.5 points
   - Sustained for 2+ weeks
   - **Response:** Enter Phase 3 consolidation (week 13+) with reduced frequency

### 4.3 Flare Management Protocol (Acute Pain Increase)

**Flare Definition:** Patient-reported pain NRS increase ≥2 points from baseline sustained >2 consecutive days, OR functionally limiting pain event.

**Flare Response Cadence (Weeks 1-2 of Flare):**
- **Days 1-3:** Daily brief check-ins (5-8 min each)
  - Assessment: What changed? Injury? Stress trigger? Overactivity?
  - Education: Review flare is temporary; nervous system is protected, not damaged
  - Adjustment: Modify exercise temporarily if appropriate; maintain some light activity
  - Reassurance: Pain intensity doesn't equal tissue damage; manageable

- **Weeks 2-3:** Increase frequency to 4-5x/week (if not already there)
  - Embedded exercise session: modify load/duration if pain limiting
  - 1-2x CBT session: focus on catastrophizing and avoidance patterns, coping strategies
  - 1x motivation/reassurance session
  - Continue full PROM assessment (weekly PROM if standard, every 2 weeks if already in low-contact phase)

- **Week 3-4:** Return to baseline frequency if flare resolves and PROM improve
- **Ongoing:** If flare persists >2 weeks, clinical escalation to Tier 3 (supervising clinician review)

## 5. Session Type Templates and Content Standards

### 5.1 Quick Check-In Session (5 minutes)
**Primary Purpose:** Brief accountability and symptom monitoring

**Standard Agenda:**
1. Pain rating (NRS 0-10, current or typical today): 30 sec
2. Exercise completion confirmation: 30 sec
   - "Did you complete the home exercises since we last spoke?"
   - If yes: "How did they feel? Any pain change?"
   - If no: "What got in the way?"
3. Brief motivational or factual statement (20 sec)
   - Sample: "You've kept this up for 3 weeks — that consistency is building new habits."
   - Sample: "Pain today doesn't reflect setback; it's just today's nervous system signal."
4. Any urgent concerns or new symptoms (1 min)
   - "Anything new or concerning since we last spoke?"
   - Red flag screening if new symptom reported
5. Preview of next session or week (30 sec)

**Documentation:** Pain NRS, exercise Y/N, adherence pattern, any new symptoms

**Optimal Timing:** Brief enough to fit into daily routine; can be text, voice, or brief video

### 5.2 Exercise Coaching Session (10-15 minutes)
**Primary Purpose:** Exercise technique refinement, progression, adherence support, pain response reassurance

**Standard Agenda:**
1. Pain and symptom check (1-2 min)
   - Current pain NRS
   - Any new symptoms since last session
   - Recent activity or pain patterns

2. Exercise review (3-5 min)
   - Video or photo review of patient's exercise performance (if submitting video)
   - OR verbal walk-through of technique ("Talk me through how you're doing the glute bridge")
   - Identify technique wins and refinement areas
   - Sample feedback: "Great neutral spine on that rep. Let's tighten the core engagement on the next one."

3. Progression discussion (3-5 min)
   - Review last session's progression or new exercise introduction
   - Assess readiness for load/duration/complexity increase
   - Offer 2-3 progression options; let patient choose based on comfort
   - Sample: "You've done 3 x 12 for 2 weeks. Ready to try 3 x 15, or would you prefer adding light weight first?"

4. Difficulty/barrier problem-solving (2-3 min)
   - "What's hardest about these exercises?"
   - Problem-solve modifications (time, space, joint pain, etc.)
   - Normalize pain response: "Mild muscle soreness 24 hr later is OK; nerve-type pain is a caution signal"

5. Pain education micro-moment (1-2 min)
   - Link exercise to pain neuroscience: "You're strengthening tissues AND re-training your nervous system that movement is safe"
   - Reassure on DOMS or mild pain response

6. Next session preview (30 sec)
   - "Next time we'll check your form again and see if you're ready for progression"

**Documentation:** Exercise adherence (Y/N, reps/sets), pain response, techniques addressed, progression offered/accepted, any modifications needed

**Best Practices:**
- Video-preferred format (patient submits video of performance, or coach demonstrates via video with feedback annotation)
- Can be text-based for simple check-in if video unavailable
- Aim for encouraging, specific feedback (not generic praise; cite specific movements observed)

### 5.3 Educational Module / Pain Neuroscience Session (15-20 minutes)
**Primary Purpose:** Deliver evidence-based pain education, CBT content, or self-management skills in digestible modules

**Standard Agenda:**
1. Connection/check-in (1-2 min)
   - "How are you feeling today? Any particular pain-related concerns?"
   - Brief pain/mood update
   - **Purpose:** Personalize module to patient's current state

2. Session objective and relevance (1-2 min)
   - "Today we're focusing on [catastrophizing patterns / nervous system states / activity pacing]. This relates to what you mentioned about [patient's specific concern]."
   - **Purpose:** Activate prior knowledge and demonstrate relevance

3. Core educational content delivery (8-12 min)
   - Micro-module with clear, digestible concepts
   - Use analogies, visuals, or storytelling where possible
   - Interactive elements: "What do you think happens to your nervous system when you catastrophize?" (elicit patient's idea, then validate/expand)
   - **Typical structure:**
     - Concept introduction (2 min)
     - Explanation with example (3-4 min)
     - Linkage to patient's pain/experience (2-3 min)
     - Interactive reflection or question (1-2 min)

4. Practice assignment or reflection (2-3 min)
   - Concrete task for patient to do between sessions
   - Sample PNE assignment: "This week, notice 3 times when you catastrophize about pain. Write them down; we'll review next time."
   - Sample CBT assignment: "Try this thought reframing when you notice 'I can't do anything.' Ask yourself: 'Have I truly not done anything, or am I pacing differently?'"
   - Sample self-management assignment: "Try the pacing strategy with one activity this week (e.g., gardening, work task). Rate difficulty before and after."

5. Summary and key takeaway (1-2 min)
   - Restate main concept in 1-2 sentences
   - Sample: "Your nervous system learns from graduated challenges. Small, consistent movements tell it that movement is safe."
   - Connect to pain goal: "This directly supports your goal to reduce pain catastrophizing and build confidence."

6. Questions and clarification (1-2 min)
   - "What questions come up? Anything unclear?"
   - Normalize confusion; offer to revisit next session if needed

**Documentation:** Topic covered, patient understanding (check for comprehension via question response), assignment given, any knowledge gaps noted

**PNE Module Curriculum (Progressive)**
- **Module 1 (Week 1-2):** Neuroplasticity & Pain Neuroscience Basics
  - Pain is a learned response produced by the nervous system, not tissue damage signal
  - Brain perceives threat → pain experience; brain perceives safety → less pain

- **Module 2 (Week 2-3):** Central Sensitization & Protective Nervous System
  - Chronic pain involves nervous system amplification (wind-up)
  - Protective nervous system = nervous system perceiving threat (even if not objectively present)

- **Module 3 (Week 3-4):** Autonomic Nervous System & Pain
  - Sympathetic dominance (stress, pain, immobility) vs. parasympathetic (recovery, healing, movement confidence)
  - Breathing, relaxation, gentle movement activate parasympathetic

- **Module 4 (Week 4):** Movement Retraining & Safety Signals
  - Gradual movement progression sends "safety" signals to nervous system
  - Pain during movement doesn't mean danger; can build confidence through graduated challenge

- **Modules 5-6 (Week 5-8):** Pain-Stress-Catastrophizing Loop
  - Catastrophizing (expecting worst) signals danger to nervous system
  - Stress and threat perception amplify pain
  - Realistic appraisal and cognitive flexibility reduce pain signal

- **Modules 7-8 (Week 8-12):** Relapse Prevention & Long-term Self-Management
  - Setbacks are expected; early action prevents relapse
  - Self-management identity (seeing self as capable pain manager) sustains change
  - Built-in strategies and supports prevent reversion to protective patterns

**CBT Content Curriculum (Progressive)**
- **Session 1 (Week 3):** Thought Monitoring & Recognition of Pain-Related Thinking
  - "I'll never get better" / "This pain means my body is broken" / "I can't do anything"
  - Identify automatic thoughts triggered by pain

- **Session 2 (Week 3):** Cognitive Restructuring for Pain-Related Thoughts
  - Challenge catastrophic thoughts with evidence
  - "What evidence do I have that I CAN't do this? What have I done recently that contradicts that thought?"

- **Session 3 (Week 6):** Behavioral Activation & Activity Scheduling
  - "Boom-bust" cycle: overactivity when feeling better, collapse when pain returns
  - Steady, paced activity prevents relapse; matches nervous system recovery pace

- **Session 4 (Week 7):** Acceptance & Commitment to Values
  - "I can have pain AND move toward what matters to me"
  - Values clarification (work, relationships, leisure, self-care)
  - Committed action despite pain (pain-neutral vs. pain-avoidant)

- **Session 5 (Week 10):** Relapse Prevention & Coping Strategies
  - Identify high-risk situations (stress, inactivity, negative mood)
  - Build coping strategy menu: "When X happens, I will Y"
  - Practice activation of strategies before crisis

### 5.4 Comprehensive Review / Progress Monitoring Session (20-30 minutes)
**Primary Purpose:** Administer full PROM battery, visualize progress, assess phase readiness, adjust care plan

**Standard Agenda:**

1. Opening and context-setting (1-2 min)
   - "We're doing a full check-in today to see how the program is working and make any adjustments"

2. PROM Administration (8-12 min)

3. Trend Visualization (3-5 min)

   - Sample visual: Pain NRS trend over weeks; PROMIS PF T-score trend
   - Highlight wins: "Look, your pain is trending down 0.3 points per week on average"
   - Acknowledge plateaus: "You're holding steady here, which is OK — consolidation phase"
   - Flag deterioration: "We see a slight uptick this week. Let's talk about what changed"

4. Goal and Care Plan Reassessment (5-7 min)
   - Review original goals from intake: "You started wanting to reduce pain and return to gardening. How are we doing?"
   - Discuss interim accomplishments: "You've been consistent with exercises for 8 weeks — that's major"
   - Adjust goals as needed: "Given your progress, should we add a new goal around [work activities / strength / etc.]?"
   - Assess readiness for phase transition (if applicable)

     - **Phase 3→4 criteria:** Pain + function MCID sustained, catastrophizing improving, self-efficacy improving, patient requests reduced frequency
     - **Phase 4 completion criteria:** Pain MCID durability confirmed, self-management competency demonstrated, patient reports readiness for independence or lower-touch support

5. Care Plan Adjustment (2-4 min)

     - **Exercise program:** progression, duration, frequency, format
     - **Education focus:** deepen specific areas (e.g., "catastrophizing is still high; let's do more CBT"), or complete curriculum sections
     - **Session structure:** increase/decrease frequency, adjust session types, modify timing
   - **Shared decision-making:** "Based on your progress, I'd suggest [X]. Does that feel right to you, or would you prefer [Y]?"

6. Next Phase Preview (1-2 min)
   - If approaching phase transition: "Next week we're transitioning to Phase 3, which focuses more on self-management. Here's what will change..."
   - Set expectation: "Phase 3 will be less frequent but more focused on helping you lead your own program"

7. Motivational Closing (1-2 min)
   - Personalized affirmation based on progress: "Your consistency over 12 weeks shows real commitment to your health. That's the foundation of lasting change."
   - Reinforce self-efficacy: "You're not just getting better pain scores — you're building the skills to manage pain long-term."

8. Questions and Feedback (1-2 min)
   - "How does this plan feel? Questions or concerns?"
   - Invite patient input on any aspect of program

**Documentation:** Full PROM scores, trend analysis, phase readiness assessment, care plan adjustments, patient feedback/concerns, next phase preview

**Frequency:**
- Phase 1: Week 4 (end of phase)
- Phase 2: Week 8 and Week 12 (midpoint and end of phase)

**Output/Deliverable:** Shared PROM summary document or visual dashboard (printable or shareable format) with trends, goals, and next steps

## 6. Re-engagement Protocol and Missed Session Escalation

### 6.1 Missed Session Categories and Response

**Single Missed Session (First Time)**
- **Automated response (within 4 hours):** Gentle reminder text/email
  - Sample: "Hi [Patient], we had a session scheduled today. No worries if something came up — reply with a new time that works."
  - Offer rescheduling flexibility

- **No response within 24 hours:** Follow-up message
  - Sample: "Just checking in — everything OK? We can reschedule to any time this week."

- **Response + reschedule:** Resume normal cadence; note one-time barrier in chart if shared

- **No response + no reschedule:** Move to "Mild Disengagement" protocol (see 6.2)

---

### 6.2 Mild Disengagement (2-3 Consecutive Missed Sessions)

**Day 1 of Disengagement:**
- **Gentle check-in message** (text, email, or brief call if phone option available)
  - Sample: "Hi [Patient], we've missed you for a couple sessions — everything OK? Something about the program not working, or just life getting busy? I'm here to help adjust however needed."
  - Tone: Supportive, non-judgmental, warm
  - **Purpose:** Open door for honest feedback; signal coach cares

**Day 3:**
- **Motivational message with progress reminder**
  - Sample: "Wanted to remind you of your progress — you've reduced pain by 2 points over 4 weeks, and you mentioned your goal to return to gardening. You're on track. What do you need from me to get back on board?"
  - Reference specific PROM wins and patient's stated goals
  - **Purpose:** Remind of progress to combat hopelessness/motivation loss

**Day 5:**
- **Offer program modification**
  - Sample: "If the current frequency is too much, we can scale back to [option] — whatever keeps you engaged. Or if something about the program isn't working, let me know and we'll adjust. What would help?"
  - Specific frequency options:
    - Reduce from 4x/week to 2x/week + async homework
    - Shift session times to better fit schedule
    - Change session type (shorter check-ins vs. comprehensive reviews)
  - **Purpose:** Remove barriers to engagement; show flexibility

**By Day 7:**
- **If patient re-engages:** Resume normal cadence; document barrier (schedule, comprehension, pain increase, etc.) to inform future adjustments
- **If patient remains unresponsive:** Move to "Moderate Disengagement" protocol (see 6.3)

---

### 6.3 Moderate Disengagement (4-7 Consecutive Missed Sessions)

**Follow mild protocol actions above, then:**

**Day 7:**
- **Structured barrier assessment via motivational interviewing**
  - Phone call or video message (more personal than text)
  - Sample script: "I've noticed we haven't connected in a couple weeks. I want to understand what's getting in the way. Walk me through your last few weeks — what's been different?"
  - **Listen for:** Schedule conflict, pain worsening, overwhelm, lack of confidence in program, depression/hopelessness, external stressor
  - **Document:** Specific barriers identified

**Day 10:**
- **Develop barrier-specific action plan**
  - If schedule conflict: Negotiate new session time; offer flexibility in frequency or format
  - If pain worsening: Reassess for flare; consider escalating to clinical review (see 6.4)
  - If overwhelm: Simplify program (reduce exercise complexity, consolidate education, reduce frequency)
  - If lack of confidence: Review early wins; do intensive MI session on self-efficacy and small achievable goals
  - If depression/hopelessness: Screen for depression (PHQ-9); consider clinical escalation and mental health referral
  - If external stressor: Acknowledge stress-pain link; offer stress management coping strategies; offer temporary program adjustment
  - **Shared decision-making:** "Here's what I hear: [barrier]. To help you re-engage, I'm suggesting [plan]. Does that feel doable?"

**Day 14:**
- **Clinical flag for care team awareness**
  - Alert supervising clinician (Tier 3 review): Patient has missed 5+ sessions; barrier identified and action plan in place
  - **Trigger escalation to Tier 2 (RN care coordinator) if:** Patient expresses depression, pain significantly worsened, patient requests to withdraw

- **Option to restart with simplified program**
  - Sample: "How about we restart this week with just 2 sessions per week for a month, focused on whichever part of the program feels most important to you? We can build back up from there."
  - Removes complexity; shows commitment to meeting patient where they are

---

### 6.4 Severe Disengagement (>7 Consecutive Missed Sessions / Patient Withdrawal Request)

**Immediate Actions:**

1. **Clinical team escalation (Tier 3)**
   - Alert supervising clinician + care coordinator
   - Review clinical context: pain status, comorbidities, medication changes, life stressors
   - **Decision point:** Is this disengagement or legitimate clinical deterioration?

2. **Phone outreach from care coordinator** (within 48 hours)
   - Warm call; express genuine concern: "I noticed we haven't heard from you. We care about how you're doing. Can we talk about what's happening?"
   - **If patient answers:** Use motivational interviewing; explore barriers; offer reframing: "You haven't failed. Life happens. How can we make this work?"
   - **If patient unreachable:** Try 2-3 times over 1 week; document attempts

3. **Reassess program fit**
   - **Is Kimi the right program for this patient?**
     - Severe psychiatric comorbidity requiring specialist mental health treatment (not AI coaching)
     - Chronic pain related to active medical condition requiring different management approach
     - Patient preference for human coaching or in-person rehabilitation (refer to local PT/pain management)
     - Unstable housing, transportation barriers, or other social determinants preventing engagement

4. **Options at this juncture:**
   - **Option A - Program pause:** "Let's pause Kimi for 3 months. Focus on stabilizing [depression/pain/life situation]. We can reconnect when things settle. Here's a referral to [mental health provider / PT]."
   - **Option B - Simplified micro-program:** "Let's do the absolute minimum: one brief check-in per week, and one exercise. That's it. We're keeping the door open, but we won't overwhelm you."
   - **Option C - Transition to different support:** Referral to in-person pain management, chronic pain specialty clinic, or mental health provider if more appropriate
   - **Option D - Patient-initiated withdrawal:** If patient explicitly requests to stop: Honor request professionally; provide written resource summary for future reference; offer re-engagement pathway ("We're here if you want to come back")

5. **Documentation and transition planning**
   - Document final PROM if available (for outcome tracking and future re-engagement context)
   - Summarize patient's progress-to-date and self-management skills developed
   - Provide hand-off to any referred provider: "Here's what we worked on; here's where [patient] found success"
   - Calendar reminder (60-90 days): Outreach check-in if paused: "Just checking in on how you're doing. Ready to restart when you are."

### 7.1 PROM Battery and Administration Schedule

**Baseline PROM (Week 1, Phase 1):**

- OARSI Function Scale (functional activities relevant to MSK)
- PHQ-9 (depression screening; 0-27 score)

**Ongoing PROM Frequency:**
- **Weekly (Phases 1-2):** 3-item brief battery (Pain NRS, single disability item, single confidence item)
  - Administration time: ~2 min
  - Administered during weekly progress review session

- **Every 2 weeks (Phases 3-4):** 3-item brief battery
  - Maintain close symptom monitoring during transition phases

- **Monthly full battery (Phase 3 onward):** Full PROM battery every 4 weeks
  - Time-intensive; schedule during comprehensive review sessions

- **Quarterly full battery (Phase 4):** Weeks 28, 36, 44, 52
  - Confirm sustained MCID and assess long-term trajectory

### 7.2 MCID Target Thresholds and Monitoring

**Primary MCID Targets (Expected 8-12 Week Achievement):**

| Outcome | Baseline Average | MCID Target | Threshold | Timeline | Trigger if Missed |
|---------|------------------|-------------|-----------|----------|------------------|
| Pain Intensity (NRS) | 6.5/10 | ≥30% reduction | NRS ≤4.5 | Week 12 | Flare protocol; increase CBT/PNE |
| Physical Function (PROMIS PF T-score) | 35 | +2.5 T-score points | T≥37.5 | Week 12 | Exercise program review; assess barriers |
| Kinesiophobia (TAMPA Scale) | 42 | ≥12-point reduction | ≤30 | Week 16 | Increase PNE; graded movement protocol |
| Self-Efficacy (PSEQ) | 28 | +6 points | ≥34 | Week 12 | Increase exercise coaching; build self-management |
| Catastrophizing (PCS) | 38 | ≥10-point reduction | ≤28 | Week 16 | Increase CBT sessions; address negative thinking patterns |

**MCID Achievement Tracking:**
- **30-day milestone (Week 4):** First signal of improvement (≥10% pain reduction)
  - Celebrate progress ("Your nervous system is responding"); reinforce program adherence
  - If no signal: Review barriers; increase intensity if appropriate

- **90-day milestone (Week 12):** Expected MCID achievement for exercise-responsive patients
  - Pain + function MCID should be achieved or within reach
  - If not achieved: Reassess diagnosis/classification; consider specialist referral for atypical pain syndrome
  - If achieved: Reinforce learning; transition to Phase 3 consolidation

- **180-day milestone (Week 26):** Expected MCID achievement for biopsychosocial interventions (pain + function + catastrophizing + self-efficacy)
  - All primary MCID targets should be moving toward achievement
  - If lagging: Increase psychoeducational content; deepen behavior change work

- **365-day milestone (Week 52):** Long-term maintenance confirmation
  - Assess durability of MCID gains (sustained for past 4 weeks)
  - Document as successful outcome or identify relapse risk factors

### 7.3 Engagement Metrics and Quality Indicators

**Track Weekly:**
- Session completion rate (target: ≥75%)
- Response latency (time from session scheduled to patient engagement)
- Time-in-session (duration patient engages vs. scheduled)
- Exercise adherence (completion of prescribed exercises)
- Homework completion (patient completes assigned tasks between sessions)

**Correlate with PROM:**
- Engagement rate vs. pain trajectory (expected: higher engagement → better pain outcomes)
- Exercise completion vs. function improvements
- Session attendance patterns vs. PROM scores (identify early disengagement signals)

**Monthly Quality Review:**
- % of patients achieving phase milestones on schedule
- % of patients achieving MCID on expected timeline
- Adverse event rate (worsening, safety concerns)
- Re-engagement rate (% re-engaged after disengagement)
- Patient satisfaction with cadence and content (short post-phase surveys)

## 9. Special Populations and Cadence Modifications

### 9.1 Older Adults (Age ≥75)

**Modifications:**
- **Phase 1 extended to 5-6 weeks** if cognitive complexity high or technology adoption slow
- **Session duration shorter:** 5-10 min preferred (15 min maximum)
- **Larger text and simpler language:** Read at 5th-6th grade reading level for accessibility
- **Phone-preferred over app:** Offer phone call sessions in addition to text/app
- **Exercise progression more conservative:** Smaller load increases (25% vs. 50% increments), focus on functional movements (sit-to-stand, reaching, walking)
- **Fall risk assessment:** Include balance screening; modify exercise if balance impaired
- **Medication interactions:** Assess opioid use and deprescribing opportunity; monitor cognitive side effects of pain medications

### 9.2 Patients with Comorbid Depression/Anxiety (PHQ-9 ≥15 or GAD-7 ≥10)

**Modifications:**
- **Parallel mental health referral:** Refer to mental health provider concurrent with Kimi program (not sequential)
  - Pain and mood are bidirectional; addressing both simultaneously more effective than sequential
- **Phase 1 extended to 5 weeks** with increased motivational check-ins and psychoeducation on pain-mood connection
- **Increase CBT content:** Dedicating extra sessions to mood-related cognitive restructuring and behavioral activation for depression
- **Monitor for suicidality:** PHQ-9 item 9 (suicidal ideation) flagged for clinical review if score ≥2
  - If suicidal ideation present: Escalate to Tier 3 clinical review; consider temporary program pause pending psychiatric evaluation
- **Medication awareness:** Inquire about antidepressant/anxiolytic use and coordination with mental health provider

### 9.3 Patients with Limited Literacy or Digital Skills

**Modifications:**
- **Phone-first delivery:** Prioritize phone calls over text/app-based sessions
- **Oral administration of PROM:** Read questions aloud; record responses verbally
- **Simplified exercise instructions:** Demonstration video with voiceover; minimal text
- **Plain language:** Avoid medical jargon; define terms used (e.g., "catastrophizing = imagining the worst")
- **Visual aids:** Use simple diagrams or illustrations for pain neuroscience concepts
- **Teach digital skills:** If patient willing, brief tutorials on app use (video calls, submitting exercises, etc.)
- **Flexible session timing:** Offer sessions via phone at times convenient for work/caregiving

### 9.4 Patients with Severe Baseline Pain or Disability

**Modifications:**
- **Phase 1 extended to 5-6 weeks** with focus on pain management and small movement confidence-building
- **Exercise begins with very gentle movement:** Range-of-motion only; no load; focus on nervous system education ("movement is safe") before progression
- **Increase pain neuroscience education:** Spend extra sessions on protective nervous system and pain amplification; normalize high pain despite low tissue damage
- **Frequent brief check-ins:** Daily check-ins may be warranted for first 6-8 weeks to build safety and support
- **Sleep and stress management:** Prioritize sleep hygiene and stress reduction techniques early (can reduce pain intensity 10-20%)
- **Consider pain management specialist coordination:** If pain refractory to standard approaches by week 8, flag for orthopedic or pain management consultation

## 10. Implementation Summary: AI Agent Scheduling Logic

### 10.1 Decision Tree for Cadence Assignment

**At enrollment:**
1. Assess patient stratification (age, baseline pain, disability, psychiatric comorbidity, digital skills)
2. Assign to HIGH / STANDARD / LOW intensity cadence
3. Set Phase 1 start date and frequency
4. Input all baseline PROM
5. **Generate 12-week calendar** with all Phase 1 + Phase 2 sessions pre-scheduled

**Weekly (Automated Review):**
1. Calculate engagement score (session completion, response latency, homework completion)
2. Check PROM trend:
   - Improvement trend? Continue current cadence
   - Flat/worsening? Trigger escalation (increase frequency, add motivational session, assess barriers)
3. Check adherence:
   - ≥75%? Continue current phase
   - 50-74%? Add 1 motivational session/week; assess barriers
   - <50%? Trigger mild re-engagement protocol
4. Flag for clinical review if:
   - PROM significantly worsens (NRS ≥2 points above baseline)
   - Patient reports new/concerning symptoms
   - Depression or anxiety worsening (PHQ-9 ≥20)
   - Non-adherence pattern (≥3 consecutive missed sessions)

**At phase transitions (Weeks 4, 12, 24, 52):**
1. Administer full PROM battery
2. Assess phase transition readiness:
   - Phase 1→2: Baseline collected, safety cleared, habit formed?
   - Phase 2→3: MCID achieved or on track? Exercise confidence ≥75%?
   - Phase 3→4: Secondary MCID tracking well? Self-management competency ≥80%?
   - Phase 4 completion: MCID durable? Self-efficacy sustained?
3. **Decision:** Move to next phase OR extend current phase with modified plan
4. Generate next phase calendar with adjusted frequency/content
5. Send patient notification: "You've reached [milestone]. Here's what phase [X] looks like..."

### 10.2 System Integration Points

**PROM Data Flow:**
- Week 1: Baseline PROM auto-loaded into system → triggers Phase 1 schedule generation
- Weekly: Brief PROM captured during session → auto-calculates trend; flags if <-2 point change
- Monthly: Full PROM → auto-calculates vs. MCID targets; generates patient-facing progress report
- Week 52: Final PROM → generates 12-month summary; auto-submits CMS outcome data

**Engagement Tracking:**
- Each session completion logged (timestamp, duration, response provided Y/N)
- Missed session → auto-trigger mild re-engagement protocol (day 1-7 messages)
- 3+ consecutive misses → escalate to moderate protocol (clinical flag)
- 7+ consecutive misses → escalate to severe protocol (care coordinator review)

**Clinical Escalation Workflow:**
- **Tier 1 (AI System):** Automated re-engagement messages, PROM trend monitoring, engagement alerts
- **Tier 2 (RN Care Coordinator):** Phone outreach for moderate/severe disengagement; barrier assessment; program modification
